What should I expect from a ski chalet holiday?

Whether you’re hitting the slopes with your mates, treating your team to a corporate retreat or taking the family on a winter escape, make it a memorable one with an epic ski chalet holiday.
Catered ski chalet packages are definitely the way to go if you’re travelling in a group. As well as staying in a top-notch ski chalet within easy reach of the pistes, most packages will include your flights and airport transfer, too.
You don’t have to give up any of your home comforts, either. In addition to a cosy room to bed down in after a tiring day of skiing (and schnapps), most chalets come with catering. Fuel up with a hot or continental breakfast in the morning, enjoy freshly baked cakes for afternoon tea, and then tuck into a three or four-course dinner whipped up by your chalet hosts while you relax with an aperitif.
If you’ve got cash to splash, premium chalets will even throw in luxury extras like welcome champagne, pre-dinner canapés and separate meals earlier in the evening for kids. Hoping to sample some local grub? You’ll find most chalets will create regional cuisine, from fondues to French tartelettes. Chalet staff typically have one day off every week, too, giving you the chance to venture out of your cosy hideaway for food and après

You’ll find the best range of skiing chalets in France, although there’s also plenty to choose from in Switzerland and Austria. Booking a ski chalet package that includes transport is a great way to save money and avoid the stressful process of organising your own flights and transfer (especially if you’re travelling in a big group).

A lot of chalets are only available as part of a package where you’ll be booked onto a specific flight and shared transfer, however, some can be rented individually which makes sense if your group are travelling from different airports or countries. We can take care of the fiddly details for you, though, and put together a personalised chalet package.

 

You definitely won’t go hungry on a catered ski chalet holiday – even if you have specific dietary requirements. Most chalet chefs will accommodate special requests if they’re given plenty of notice, whether that’s veggie, vegan or gluten free options or you have an allergy. Tell your packed travel planner about any food preferences when you’re booking your trip and we’ll get it sorted.

 

There are few better experiences than unclipping your skis after a day on the slopes and soaking your weary limbs in warm, bubbling water. The best chalets often include amazing premium extras like hot tubs and cosy open fireplaces and private bars so you can continue the party well after the pubs and clubs close...

 

Deciding on the right ski holiday chalet for your group will be your first (and only) hurdle if you plan your trip through packed. Chalets come in all shapes and sizes, from small private chalets that sleep up to six people to those that can cater to upwards of 30 snow-seekers. It’s not uncommon to be sharing a bigger chalet with other like-minded skiers, which can be a fun and very sociable experience!

You’ll also discover chalet complexes with shared luxury amenities like spas and pools, plus ski in ski out chalets that ensure you’re the first ones making tracks in the fresh powder each morning.

Can I book a short ski chalet break?

While most chalet companies have a minimum stay of a week, it’s still possible to nab a good deal for a long weekend at certain resorts, including Chamonix and Morzine. You’re more likely to find catered ski chalets for shorter breaks between January and March, however, we recommend avoiding planning your ski weekend during busy school holiday times.

What about really big groups?

If you’re planning a big group ski trip with friends or colleagues, early booking is key. You’ll often be able to secure a whole chalet for a deposit as small as £150pp (the rest is paid nearer your trip time) if you book far enough ahead.
Planning something several months in advance and securing deposits from 20 or more people can be a real nightmare! Luckily, that’s where we come in. Instead of one person in your group being saddled with chasing payments, our travel planners will do it all for you and, where possible, even hold your chalet for a few days in case any of your group are late with their deposits.
