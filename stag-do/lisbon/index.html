Combining balmy bar crawls with adrenaline-rushing activities and some truly world-class beaches, Lisbon stag dos are in a league of their own.
Set among stunning rolling hills and boasting both river and sea views, the hip Portuguese capital is a real crowd pleaser. Whether the group’s interested in checking out the city’s cultural hotspots, hitting the sand at a buzzing beach resort or making the most of the epic bar culture in Bairro Alto, Lisbon has all your stag do shenanigans covered.
We’ll be here every step of the way to create the ideal itinerary for your group, too. From nabbing the best hotels to getting you into the hottest clubs, let us suss out your ultimate lads trip to Lisbon.

Neighbourhoods not to be missed

Bairro Alto

Lisbon’s most bohemian neighbourhood that’s known for its twisting lanes and bright yellow trams, and dozens of small, shabby-chic drinking holes where punters tend to spill outside, turning the whole area into a huge street party.

Chiado

The more sophisticated cousin of Bairro Alto, Chiado is the city’s theatre and shopping district which is packed with elegant buildings and some of the city’s top museums and galleries. You’ll also find some of the best miradouros (viewpoints) in the city.

Baixa

Sandwiched between Chiado and Alfama, Baixi is essentially Lisbon’s heart. It’s made up of wide boulevards and impressive plazas which lead all the way down to the Tagus Estuary. Hit up the shops along Avenida de Liberdade, savour local bites at the Time Out food market or visit major landmarks like the Jardim de São Pedro de Alcântara.

Stag accommodation in Lisbon

Hotel in the heart of the bar district or apartment overlooking the ocean? There’s no shortage of ace accommodation in Portugal’s capital - and we’re here to find the ideal lodgings for your Lisbon stag weekend.

If your priorities lie more with where you’ll be drinking than where you’ll be sleeping, a centrally-placed hostel with a sociable vibe could be the answer. For stags who appreciate the finer things in life, a posh hotel (think spas and sky-high cocktail bars) in the Lisbon hills might be more appealing.

And then there’s those self-catering options that give you plenty of room to spread out and allow the whole group to stick together.

Here’s a slice of what's on offer to give you an idea:

  • On a budget
  • A little extra luxury
  • A lot more luxury
  • Self-catering heroes

Safestay

Positioned on the edge of the popular Bairro Alto district, Safestay stands out on the street thanks to its bright pink exteriors. Indoors, you’ll find basic but comfortable dorms and comfy shared living areas (there’s even a sun-soaked terrace perfect for a few pints).

The Independente

Unlike your typical hostel, The Independente is housed in an impressive Art Deco mansion with sky-high ceilings and a grand entrance hall that might make you think you’ve stumbled into the wrong building. Choose from its roomy dorms complete with triple bunk beds or lock down private rooms with balconies overlooking the river.

Exe Liberdade

Stylish, modern rooms and a position just minutes from picturesque Eduardo VII Park and Lisbon’s most famous shopping street makes Exe Liberdade a great option for those after convenience and comfort without having to shell out the big bucks.

Pestana CR7

Stay seconds from the sea at this affordable 4-star hotel in between central Baixa and Chiado. It’s the joint venture of the Pestana Hotel Group and national treasure Cristiano Ronaldo, with sleek ensuite rooms which are definitely fit for football royalty.

Corinthia Hotel Lisbon

If you’ve got the budget to splash out a little, you can’t go too far wrong with the Corinthia Hotel Lisbon. Its location in hilly Campolide affords its plush suites awesome cityscape views, while the 5-star spa is the ultimate hangover hangout...

Tivoli Avenida

This 5-star gem is located right on the famous Avenida de Liberdade and its stylishly designed rooms with walk-in showers, Krup coffee machines and city outlooks aren’t its only allures. Whizz up in the lift to SEEN, one of the coolest eating spots in the city that’s connected to Tivoli Avenida’s iconic Sky Bar.

LisbonBreaks

Take your pick of over 70 apartments scattered from central Chiado to coastal Carcavelos. Whether you’re after sea views or a location right in the centre of the action, LisbonBreaks offers spacious and trendy options that’ll suit the stags down to the ground.

Chiado Arty Flats

For groups keen to stay in the thick of it, Chiado Arty Flats in Bairro Alto should be top of your list. It’s found in the former headquarters of the República newspaper and its roomy penthouses and deluxe apartments feature incredible graffiti artwork and upcycled furnishings.

The best of Lisbon

  • What’s the culture like
  • Eating
  • Drinking
  • Getting around
 

It’s not an overstatement to say that stag dos in Lisbon really do have it all. As a city with over two thousand years of history, it’s full of jaw-dropping monuments and attractive cobbled streets which make the ideal backdrops for holiday snaps suitable for your gran.

Being so close to the sea and having ace weather for most of the year additionally makes Lisbon an ideal destination for downtime. Nearby resorts like Sintra and Costa da Caparica offer up both fantastic surf and beach clubs where you can drink right on the sand.

Despite a pretty laid back atmosphere in the day, Lisbon city centre turns into a real party place afterdark thanks to its affordable booze and relaxed open container laws.

 

From cheap, rustic eats to Michelin-star dining, Lisbon’s food scene is diverse. Local taverns like Tasca do Vigário in Alfama (don’t miss the bifanas: succulent roast pork sandwiches) are perfect for authentic eats. You’ll also find mouth-watering pasteis de natas in cafes across the city, although Pastéis de Belém claims to be the original creators of the creamy, cinnamon-spiced tarts.

Seafood fans won’t be disappointed in Lisbon, either. Try local favourite bacalhau à Brás (salted cod cooked up with eggs, potatoes and onions) or tuck into some of the freshest prawns and sardines you’ll probably ever eat.

 

Whether you’re wine aficionados or keen to get ruined on tequila shots at an underground electro club, there’s ample places to let your hair down in Lisbon.

While Porto may lay claim to Portugal’s eponymous national drink, you’ll still find plenty of bars serving up the heady tipple in Lisbon. Craft beer is also having a moment, with cool microbreweries popping up across the up-and-coming Marvila district.

Crisp vinho verde - green wine - is a staple throughout Lisbon that’s served over ice (and preferably in a deckchair on the beach). If that’s not hardcore enough, head to Bairro Alto’s tiny backstreet bars after 10pm to enjoy a round - or three - of ice-cold beers for around £2 a pint.

Stags hoping to experience Lisbon’s clubbing scene will find the best venues at the bottom end of Baixa. Lux Frágil is one of the biggest that’s even got a roof terrace for when you need some respite from the dancefloor, while MusicBox underneath the railway arches pumps out EDM tunes into the small hours.

 

Lisbon, despite being very hilly, is pretty easy to navigate, even with your beer goggles on. What’s more, it won’t cost you an arm and a leg. Public transport - metros, buses and trams - is an efficient way to travel around the city, plus they run until at least 11pm. You could also pick up Lime Electric Scooters to whizz you effortlessly up and down the steep streets.

If you’re in a big group, it’s usually more cost effective to take taxis from place to place. You’ll be looking at less than a tenner to travel from one end of the city to the other. Bargain.

Stag activities in Lisbon

Active adventures or boozy bar crawls? Whatever takes your fancy, you’ll find it in this cool city. Here are just a few Lisbon stag do ideas to get you started:
  • The Classics
  • Only in Lisbon
  • Show-stoppers
  • Shoestring stags

Bubble football

Dress up in giant inflatable bubble costumes and try to stay upright as you have a comical kick around.

Quad biking adventure

Journey out of the city and get behind the wheel of a powerful quad bike. Tackle off-road circuits or have fun racing against each other

Footgolf

Even the most anti-golfers among you can get behind this hilarious stag do activity. Instead of putting with a club, you’ll be kicking footballs into giant holes.

Outdoor karting

Test your driving skills on a high-speed outdoor go karting race track which will see you going head to head with your fellow stags.

Lisbon bar crawl

Undertake an Portuguese-style bar crawl, stopping off at trendy hole in the walls in hip Bairro Alto and Cais do Sodré.

Surfing

Make the most of beautiful beaches and catch some huge waves with a group surfing lesson along the Lisbon coastline.

Beach boat party

Take your celebrating out to sea by embarking on a booze cruise around Lisbon’s coast. Rent a private catamaran or share a boat with other like-minded party people.

Lisbon Go Cars

Soak up the city’s best sights on a self-guided tour in Lisbon Go Cars, compact yellow electric cars that zip easily up and down Lisbon’s narrow streets.

Footie tickets

See Benfica or Sporting in action by nabbing tickets to one of their home games at Estádio do Sport Lisboa e Benfica or the José Alvalade Stadium.

Port tasting

Sample some of Portugal’s finest, grape-based tipple on a boozy tasting experience at one of Lisbon’s best port bars.

Reef fishing

Catch your dinner with your bare hands (or a rod) by heading out to the nearby reef for a fishing expedition.

Coasteering

Challenge the stags to this intrepid activity that’ll see you inching your away around the rocky coastline on foot or in the water.

Kayak tour

View Lisbon and its iconic landmarks from the water on an action-packed sea kayaking tour led by an expert instructor.

Rooftop bar crawl<

A step up (literally) from a classic Lisbon bar crawl, this one will see you hopping from one incredible rooftop drinking hole to another.

Stroll around Alfama

Explore one of the city’s most attractive and oldest neighbourhoods which boasts cobbled lanes teeming with cafes, bars and key sights like São Jorge Castle.

Gorge on treats at the Time Out food market

Head to the legendary Lisbon Time Out food market for local treats whipped up by award-winning Portuguese chefs.

Visit the LxFactory

An abandoned industrial complex turned cultural hub, LxFactory near Alcantara-Mar station is the ideal spot for shopping, snacks and sipping on local beer and wine.

Hang out at the Park rooftop bar

Chill out with Lisbon’s hippest crowd at offbeat Park bar that’s located on the rooftop of a disused parking lot in Bairro Alto.

Day trip to Sintra or Cascais

Hit up one of these lively beach resorts found on the outskirts of the city for some sun, sea and chilled vinho verde.
