Don’t you think your mate deserves the type of send-off he’ll remember for the rest of his life? The type of stag that’ll have you reminiscing for years to come. Don’t you want to send him off in a city that’s flush with gorgeous beaches, gorgeous women and the sort of night clubs that would make Dan Bilzerian weak at the knees?

Benidorm may be a den of debauchery but it’s also a coastal gem, full of boozy barbeques, catamaran cruises and crystal-clear waters. The stag and hen friendly world centres around the bronzed Levante Beach, with its bars, clubs and restaurants, whilst the most peaceful Poniente Beach attracts a more mature crowd.

Whatever type of shag stag you’re after, Benidorm is the ideal location to celebrate your best mate’s last few days of singledom.

Stag accommodation in Benidorm

Whether you’re wanting to splash the cash or do things on a shoe-string, we’ll find accommodation perfect for your group size and budget.

The good thing about Benidorm is that if you’re looking for a cheap crash pad, you’ll find plenty of options that are clean, comfy and within stumbling distance of both the beach and town centre.

If you’re wanting to bunk everyone under one roof, a spacious coastal apartment might be a good option. We know – so many decisions, so little time.

Rh Sol Hotel

If we’re honest, you’ll be spoilt for choice with the many budget options that Benidorm has to offer. The likes of Rh Sol Hotel serves up crisp clean rooms and a stellar location, right by Playa de Levante. You’re also a short walk from Benidorm’s Old Town which offers historical sites, as well as plenty of buzzy bars and nightclubs. You’ll get free Wi-Fi, a balcony, T.V and A.C as standard. If you fancy a kitchenette, we can sort this for you too.

Hotel Alameda

Hotel Alameda is a modern hotel in the old town of Benidorm with recently renovated rooms, a minimalist atmosphere and an excellent personalised customer service.

Grand Luxor

Set back from the Benidorm coastline, inside the Terra Mítica Theme Park, Grand Luxor Hotel has all the bells and whistles you need. It features a seasonal outdoor pool, a top-notch fitness centre and clean open plan rooms. You’ll be happy to know that a stay here also guarantees you free unlimited access to Terra Mítica, Mundomar, Iberia Park and Aqualandia Theme Parks. All rooms come with a flat-screen TV, either a balcony or a terrace and a sofa bed as standard. Rooms sleep up to 4 people.

Melia Villaitana

There’s no doubt that this hotel oozes old school swagger. Located on the beautiful Costa Blanca coast, this holiday resort provides modern spacious rooms and more facilities than you’ll know what to do with. Make the most of the two golf courses, decked out gym, football pitches, paddle tennis courts and a brilliant spa for those mornings when the hangover is just too much. Some suits and family rooms sleep up to 6 adults.

P&V Levante

TThese apartments are simply furnished with open plan living rooms right in the thick of Levante, within minutes of lively Levante beach. For the ultimate in budget-friendly, stick a couple of the lads on the sofabed in the living room.

P&V Poniente

These simple and stylish apartments are just 1km from Benidorm’s Poniente Beach, offering all you need if you want to go self-catering. You’ll get a seasonal outdoor pool, an indoor pool, a gym, and a fully decked out lad’s games room. Plus, each apartment has its own private balcony. Swish.

How do I get the group to Benidorm?

The nearest airport to Spain’s glittering golden coastline is Alicante. Valencia and Murcia are also nearby. London, Manchester, Birmingham and Bristol are some of the best UK airports to travel from as they all offer direct flights to Alicante. Prices start from as little as £30 return, excluding any check-in baggage fees.

To save you the hassle of coordinating flight bookings, we’ll do you a solid by throwing the cost of air travel into your overall Benidorm stag do package. This leaves you free to focus on the crucial bits - like planning those all-important stag activities - rather than having to chase your mates for money.

Although big group bookings can often make flights more expensive (there’s typically only so many tickets available for a cheap fee), you can sleep easy knowing we’ll secure the best prices possible for your chosen dates and the whole group will pay the same amount. Once you touch down in Alicante, we’ll even arrange transfers into Benidorm. All you need to do is make it on to the plane.

The best of Benidorm

  • What’s the culture like
  • Eating
  • Drinking
  • Chilling
 

Benidorm is so much more than high rises and hedonistic happenings. You’ll find a historic old town, set on a hill between the two beaches as well as plenty of buzzy bars and restaurants along the main strip. Along this strip you’ll also find some of the best fry ups outside of London, strip bingo, karaoke and some damn good fish and chips. In recent years, word of the city’s cheap thrills and anything-goes attitude has made it an attractive location for stag dos and sunseekers alike.

 

You can find pretty much anything you like in this melting pot city. If you’re hankering for some good authentic Spanish fare, then head to the likes of Bar Sant Jaume, where you’ll find every type of tapas you can imagine and a drinks list as long as your arm.

You’ll be wanting to pop into Tasca y Basta for the meatballs alone. They serve homemade fare, and the type of patatas bravas that can rectify even the messiest of nights – piping hot and doused in a delicious garlicky mayonnaise.

For steaks and burgers, it has to be The Grill House where you’ll find big smoky flavours and burgers the size of Arnold Schwarzenegger’s biceps. If you’re feeling brave, double up with two 200g burgers, served with all the trimmings, thick cut chips and coleslaw.

Northern Sole, tucked down a cobbled street in Old Town, serves up the type of drool-inducing, satisfyingly salty fare that’ll cure your hangover in just one whiff. The burgers here are massive, with unusual toppings like pulled pork, onion bhaji, and even curry burgers served in naan bread. The fish and chips are notorious, better than your Northern grandma used to make.

Manage your stamina with late-night pizzas or burgers. Llevatelo’s pizzas and Snack Bar Mariano's are both safe options.

 

There are over 2000 bars in Benidorm so saying that you’ll be spoilt for choice is a bit of an understatement. Hit the street called Calle Gerona, which is chock-full of great bars and everyone’s generally in a great, sloshed mood. You’ll find club promoters dotted up and down this street and, if you play nice, they’ll sort you out with all kinds of drinks deals.

If you’re taking your Spanish stag fiesta to new heights (literally) then Amigo’s Restaurant and Roof Terrace is a great option. You can book out the upstairs and enjoy perks like unlimited Sangria, your own playlist, and a private chef and waiter. Not too shabby, eh?

As you walk around the city one bar name will just keep cropping up. That name is Stardust. It’s one of those places you can’t leave Benidorm without seeing. It’s friendly. It’s always rammed. It’s always awesome. Pull up a spot on the cosy wooden table at the back and watch as the cheesy tunes turn into a full-on strip show. You’ll never want to leave.

Grab yourself a bucket of beer at The Red Lion before heading down to Aloha Beach Bar. Whilst "fruity drinks under tiki huts" might not be the first image that comes to mind when you think "stag do”, you have to make the exception for this place.

Aloha Beach Bar has everything you need for an epic night – free flowing booze, pretty girls, beach front location. A note to the wise though – it does tend to get rowdy in the later hours. Make sure you stay out of trouble. A bachelor party spent in Benidorm? Hell yes. A bachelor party spent in a Benidorm jail? Hell no.

 

If you fancy seeing a bit of Spanish culture whilst on your trip, then Old Town is the place to go. Spend some time checking out the traditional Spanish shops and restaurants that line the narrow-cobbled alleys. If you’re in the mood for some authentically Spanish food, head to Poniente, where there’s a quiet beach and a port where you can jet-ski. Then you’ve got Levante. This place needs no introduction – it’s where the carnage happens and where happy hour never seems to end. You’ll know it when you see it. Whatever stag activities you fancy, these three places have got you covered.

Stag activities in Benidorm

Searching for unique Benidorm stag ideas? Take your pick of the following activities or get in touch if you’ve got something more specific in mind…
  • The Classics
  • Only in Benidorm
  • Show-stoppers
  • Shoestring stags

Karting

A man of refined tastes, your soon-to-be-hitched pal knows exactly what he wants and if that’s a game of Go Karting then let him have it. Test out your driving skills, negotiate the hair-pin turns, and throw in some forfeits to raise the stakes.

Bubble football

Like normal football but in a big inflatable bubble. You’ll be falling out and crashing all over the show but that’s all part of the fun. Probably best leave the drinking until after.

Terra Mitica – theme park in the sun

This park features some seriously gravity-defying roller coasters, and around 300 actors who put on all kinds of wild live shows. Oh, did we mention they also serve beer and burgers the size of your head? Yeah.

Catamaran cruise

From relaxing beach tours with a BBQ to party boats complete with free-flowing booze, Benidorm cruises can be as cultural or as clubby as you like.

Waterpark- Aqualandia

The park offers a wide range of attractions, from slides to lazy rivers to a heart-arresting twisty half-pipe that’ll cure your hangover in a jiffy.

Half Day Jeep Safari

Check out the scenic beauty and rugged terrains of the Costa Blanca Mountain Ranges on a 4-hour jeep safari. Don’t forget your driving license.

Power boat

If you have a hangover in Benidorm, get yourself down to the harbour and take a shot on the Power Boat. You’ll be doing everything from 360 degree turns to Tsunamis. It’s probably not for the faint hearted, but you only live once right?

Private yacht hire

If you’ve always fancied yourself as the bloke on the luxury yacht in the movies, then here’s your chance to live out that dream. Book your stags onto a private yacht and party as hard as you like.

Jet ski

This popular stag activity offers the perfect opportunity to raise your pulse with a spot of jet skiing. Is there a better hangover cure than blasting through the ocean at high speed? We think not.

Playa de Poniente

This gorgeous promenade is one of three beaches in Benidorm. It doesn’t cost a penny and boasts ocean views, a bustling boardwalk and plenty of flip-flops and sun burns. Pack the sunscreen and head here for a chilled one.

Stroll the Old Town (Casco Antiguo)

Head away from the crowds up to Benidorm’s Old Town where you’ll find plenty of shops, bars, and authentic Spanish places to eat. The overall vibe of this area can only be described as “chill.”

Visit Benidorm Island

Boats depart hourly to this beautiful island. Whilst there, make the most of the authentic seafood cuisine, and serene views of the Benidorm coastline.

Hike Sierra Helada Natural Park

With 300 metre cliffs and more than 5600 hectares of flora and fauna, hiking Sierra Helada is one for your Benidorm bucket list. Breathe in the fresh air and prepare for all kinds of adventure. Strap on your walking boots and get hiking.
