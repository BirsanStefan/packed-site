Friends holidays

Trips away with friends power the morale of the UK population all year round. They cheer our souls during the long dark winter months. Trips with friends puncture the bitterness of cold-weather commuting with the ski trip you have been waiting all summer and autumn for. You wait months for respite from the spreadsheets and office life – then like magic, a hen and wedding arrive at once to lift the spirits. Perhaps there is no stag to survive this year but no matter – football tour with the boys is here in May.

Mixed groups may require some consensus but they shouldn’t mean mixed results when booking a holiday. Be it a weekend away or backpacking, the same approach of engaging an on-hand travel expert to take the pain out of dealing with the details just makes sense. Friends don’t let friends drown in trip admin.

Travelling with friends can be about seeking new experiences and going outside your comfort zone with the reassurance of having someone you know loving it and living it with you every step of the way. Does it have to be something extreme like leaping off New Zealand’s Kawarau Bridge – birthplace of the bungee jump? Of course not. A delicious pizza taste tour of southern Italy is something to be shared and savoured with a friend. Just be careful about nicking that last slice if you don’t want a frosty few hours’ drive to follow.

Sports teams

Each year sports teams of every description, be they university teams, local sports clubs or schools, head overseas on a sports tour. With a huge number of logistics to be considered in a package deal, help from an expert is essential. With assistance on everything ranging from finding a suitable location – where the 14-year-old girls football team won’t have to endure nightly dinner alongside Elephant & Castle Rugby Club 3rd XV – to excursions to sports stadiums and local interest sites. The greater your numbers the more important it is that everything is tailored to how you need it to be. Don’t fail to plan and be caught offside without the right tickets once overseas.

Golf trips

A timeless pursuit as much for business as for leisure, golf tours are booming among the young and the old. And also, increasingly for the girls as well as the boys as the image of the game changes.

The options for jetting off with the irons in excess baggage are growing all the time. The traditional European golfing hotbed of Portugal’s west Algarve remains as popular as ever – boasting both Vale do Lobo and Quinta do Lago resorts just 40 minutes from Faro airport. Further afield, if you feel like you need to turn up the heat a little, both Morocco and Turkey have developed into fantastic all-round options with impressive courses. And as alien as it may be on a warm weather break, stay out of the sand and the water until you’re through all 18 holes. Thank us later.
