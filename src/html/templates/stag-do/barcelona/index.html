The biggest headache in choosing a stag destination can be pleasing all the people on the trip and pandering to their needs. But no matter how diverse your group of mates, there is no one that Barcelona doesn’t have covered. So take your pals there and expect to earn their props.
For this is a city known to de–cynic the cynic; get the tight–wad spending; the clean–living partying; and the shy having random adventures with strangers all around the city.
And let’s not to forget those who are already up for it – who can expect a truly epic blow out right across the board.

The best of Barcelona

  • Clubbing
  • Chilling
  • Eating and drinking
  • Activities
 

Music aficionados and clubbers will find some of the finest venues and DJs in Europe ‐ whether you want to go glamorous, budget or underground. And the places here are open morning, day and night.

Be it techno, hip hop or house, your bases are covered ‐ and there are plenty of places where you can be hooked up with primo tables to keep your crew together.

 

Chillers and down‐time seekers can hit up the dazzling beaches ‐ with dazzling bodies to match ‐ which line this coastal city. The stretch of sand around Port Olímpic Marina is a great spot for people‐watching and a few, languid hair‐of‐the‐dogs.

Barcelona’s beaches also play host to day‐time parties and raves for if and when you feel like switching up gears. But if you prefer to blaze your own trail and keep a distance from other stag groups, there are secret spots that Packed can guide you to. More on that below.

 

Bar flies and bar enthusiasts will find somewhere on every corner to enjoy an ice‐cold pint (or jarra) for prices starting at 3 Euros. You may also want to head to a shaded back street in the Old Town and sip chilled sherry at the counter with locals.

The rooftop bars in The Gothic Quarter are a good place to mix it up with the young, hot and trendy crowd while you enjoy the breeze coming in off the sea.

And Barcelona has earned the status of something of a mecca for foodies, whether you want fresh seafood, super‐cheap tapas (yours for around 12 Euros per head) or high‐end dining for which people make the pilgrimage from all over Europe and beyond.

 

If you feel like mixing up the merry go round of bars and clubs, then you’ve got a number of options. You could take the sting out of (or top up) your hangover with a boat trip along the coast of the Med by either renting a yacht just for the stag group or going communal on a catamaran cruise.

Should craft beers and ale be your thing, then you can tour a brewery and wash down your brew with jamon and croquettas. Football fans amongst you might not want to pass up a trip to one of the high temples of the game: Camp Nou.

Stag activities in Barcelona

From football related follies and classic boozy pastimes to activities you can only do in Barcelona, here’s a few stag do ideas to get you started.

  • The Classics
  • Only in Barcelona
  • Show-stoppers
  • Shoestring stags

Bubble football

Forget traditional footie. This Barcelona stag activity sees you pulling on giant inflatable bubble suits and having a truly comical kick around.

Barcelona bar crawl

One of the top Barcelona stag do ideas has to be a bar crawl of its diverse range of drinking holes. Visit a handful of hidden gems or stick to the city’s best-loved nightlife spots.

Go karting

Race your mates around one of the best indoor or outdoor circuits in the city when you pick go karting as your stag activity.

Mediterranean boat party

Embark on a boozie Barcelona boat party around the city’s coastline, complete with great tunes, fun people and a fully-stocked bar.

Beer tasting

Microbreweries have been popping up all over the Catalan capital for years. Why not sample some of the best beer Barcelona has to offer with a full-on tasting session?

Camp Nou tour

Groom football mad? Book a tour of Camp Nou, the home of FC Barcelona, and go behind the scenes to see where the players chill out before matches.

Private yacht hire

Ok, can be done elsewhere, but Party like an A-lister by hiring out a yacht in Barcelona for the day and set sail with a full beer fridge, some tasty tapas and ample opportunities to jump in the ocean.

Paddle boarding

Again, we’re being flexible with the “only in Barcelona” category. Challenge the stags to stay upright above the balmy Med on a paddle boarding tour off one of Barcelona’s picturesque beaches.

Football match

Book tickets for the whole group to watch the legendary FC Barcelona team in action at their Camp Nou hub.

Flyboard experience

If you thought paddle boarding was tricky, try staying above water on a hoverboard! This adrenaline-packed stag do activity will see you all gliding across the sea’s surface.

Waterpark excursion

Splash, slide and sun tan to your heart’s content with a fun-filled day out at one of Barcelona’s brilliant waterparks. It never gets old.

Helicopter ride

See the city and the surrounding golden coastline from above on an unforgettable helicopter tour of Barcelona.

Kayak or snorkelling tour

Explore stunning coves along the Catalan coastline and snorkel alongside local marine life inside underwater caves on a kayaking tour.

Hot air balloon ride

Admire the city from a different angle by booking the group on a hot air balloon ride. Celebratory champagne optional.

Catalan cooking class

From seafood-packed paella to tasty tapas bites, give the stags a lesson in authentic Catalan cuisine with a cookery class.

Tapas tour

Treat your tastebuds to some traditional Catalan cuisine (think patatas bravas, melt-in-the-mouth jamón and bombas stuffed with marinated mince) on a tapas tour of the city.

Tibidabo and Montjuïc

Take an unforgettable day trip up to Montjuïc in a cable car for some of the best views of the city. Alternatively, head to neighbouring Tibidabo that’s home to one of the region’s most exhilarating theme parks.

Park Güell

Stroll up to Barcelona’s UNESCO World Heritage Park Güell at sunset with some beers to admire panoramic views of the city and the sea.

The beach

Treat the lads to an afternoon at one of the city’s many idyllic beaches. Chill out at laidback Mar Bella beach or throw your towels down on the sand at La Barceloneta with its lively promenade and excellent people watching.

Cultural sights

Want your stag do to be a little more culture, a little less clubbing (or just a nice equal mix of both)? Check out the exterior of the city’s famed Sagrada Familia or embark on a free walking tour of the Gothic Quarter.

Stag accommodation in Barcelona

Barcelona has smart, stylish, hi-tech hotels which don’t charge the silly money - with double rooms starting at around £110 per night.

There is also all manner of apartments and other types of accommodation. The city is known for options that are cool or quirky; modern or period; luxurious or cheap. And you can get set up inside a larger space if you want to keep the stag group together under one roof.

You’ll also find some seriously sexy, high-end hotels - with the clientele to match. But even if you can’t afford to stay there, you can at least hit up their bars. And if the single blokes amongst you have game, these are the places to bring it to the beautiful people around you.

Need some pointers? Whether you’re working with a shoestring budget or money is no object, check out the following ace accommodation options for a Barcelona stag do. We can arrange others, but here’s a slice.

Here's a slice of what's on offer to give you an idea:

  • On a budget
  • A little extra luxury
  • A lot more luxury
  • Self-catering heroes

TOC Hostel

Combining a great location and comfortable rooms with a very decent price tag, TOC Hostel in the central Exiample neighbourhood is a great shout for stags on a budget. The hostel doesn’t scrimp on style in its contemporary shared or private rooms, plus there’s a cool games room, an onsite cocktail bar and even an outdoor pool area to keep you entertained.

Generator Hostel

Another wallet-friendly accommodation pick perfect for a stag do in Barcelona is the huge Generator Hostel. You’ll find it less than ten minutes away from Gaudi masterpiece Casa Milà in the popular Gràcia district. Rooms here are cool and colourful, while shared facilities include various bar and lounge areas for relaxing in with the whole group.

H10 Madison

For a touch more luxury without the huge bill, H10 Madison is ideal. The relatively new 4-star hotel is primely positioned on the edge of the Gothic Quarter and you’ll find its trendy twin rooms have plenty of creature comforts. There’s also a heated rooftop plunge pool and sun terrace for chilling out in the Spanish sunshine.

NH Collection Barcelona Podium

Also located in Barcelona’s heart, the NH Collection Barcelona Podium is an affordable choice for those hoping to fit in a little sightseeing, a lot of celebrating and even some R&R at the rooftop pool. Stay in stylish soundproofed rooms and wake up to one of the best breakfast spreads in the city - think buttery pastries, local meats and cheeses, plus made to order omelettes.

W Hotel

Send your best mate stylishly off into marital bliss by booking into the 5-star W Hotel. You’ll find it along Barceloneta Beach, with awesome views of the ocean and fantastic facilities like a lively beach club, an infinity pool and luxurious suites complete with rainfall showers and modern music systems.

Grand Central Hotel

Stay within walking distance of Barcelona’s best nightlife spots at the slick Grand Central Hotel. Rooms here come with free minibars, Nespresso coffee machines (ideal for perking you up after a big night...) and unbeatable city views. It’s also home to one of the city’s top rooftop bars (complete with jaw-dropping infinity pool) which regularly hosts some of Barcelona’s most exclusive parties.

Midtown Apartments Barcelona

If you’d prefer to have your own place to party (and pass out), Midtown Apartments Barcelona a stone’s throw from Plaza Catalunya can sleep between two to eight stags. Each apartment is kitted out with smart TVs, bluetooth speakers, well-equipped kitchens and roomy lounge/dining areas. Fancy a swim? Head to the building’s shared rooftop pool.

Paseo de Gràcia Bas Apartments Barcelona

A perfect position ten minutes’ walk from the Gothic Quarter and Las Ramblas isn’t the only benefit of this Barcelona accommodation option. The contemporary Paseo de Gràcia Bas Apartments also sleep up to four people, making them ideal for smaller stag do groups. Keep it basic or upgrade to one of the premium apartments which feature outstanding outdoor terraces.
