From cultural landmarks and unforgettable experiences to one of the most unique bar scenes in Europe, Budapest ticks all the boxes when it comes to your best mate’s big send off.

It would be impossible to book a stag weekend in Budapest without visiting the old Jewish Quarter (aka District Seven), either. Located on the Pest side of the river, it’s famed for its numerous ruin bars - derelict buildings turned staggeringly good drinking holes.

Stag accommodation in Budapest

No matter your group size or budget, Budapest always delivers when it comes to stag-friendly accommodation.

Those travelling on the cheap will find numerous hostels with cool facilities and unbeatable city centre locations (by that, we mean they’re right on the doorstep of Budapest’s best bars). If you’re looking for a little more luxury, the commercial centre is filled with sleek shops, trendy cocktail bars and 5-star hotels with lush spas and swimming pools to boot.

For those hoping to bunk all the stags under one roof, a spacious city centre apartment could be the ideal solution.

Wombats City Hostel

Located midway between the river and the Jewish Quarter, this lively hostel is the ideal spot to base yourself for a rowdy weekend away in Budapest. Check into Wombats City Hostel’s clean, comfortable rooms and enjoy easy access to all four corners of the city from the nearby Deák Ferenc Tér bus and tram interchange.

Meininger Budapest Great Market Hall

Right in the centre, right next to the Great Market Hall (surprisingly). Meininger is a state of the art hotel with a relaxed atmosphere, games room, B&B options and rooms that range in size from twin rooms up to 6-bed dorms. Clean, modern, damn comfortable!

Ibis Styles Hotel

For a slightly more upmarket option that’s still well within your price range, the minimalist yet modern rooms at Ibis Style Hotel are a great compromise. You’ll find a handful of these hotels in Budapest, from one in the Jewish Quarter to one near Petőfi Bridge that’s surrounded by pubs and jazz bars.

Mercure Budapest Korona

After private, comfortable ensuite rooms for a decent price? Check out the Mercure Budapest Korona in the Inner City district that’s within easy walking distance of the river and Liberty Bridge (it leads to must-see Gellért Hill).

Aria Hotel Budapest

Boldly proclaiming itself the number one hotel in Central Europe, the Aria Hotel Budapest serves up luxe rooms (think king size beds, Nespresso coffee machines and in-room iPads) as well as one of the best rooftop bars in the city. It couldn’t be better positioned for visits to St. Stephen’s Basilica, plus it’s just a few minutes’ walk from the riverfront.

Corinthia Hotel

For stags on their best behaviour, the posh Corinthia Hotel on the edge of the Jewish Quarter is a no-brainer. It’s found in one of the city’s most elegant 19th century buildings and was formerly known as the Royal Hotel Budapest. Select to stay in its luxurious suites and benefit from complimentary access to the exclusive Executive Lounge.

Budapest Harmony Apartments

Get the best of both worlds when it comes to iconic heritage sites and sensational nightlife with Budapest Harmony Apartments’ offerings. The company’s larger rentals sleep between six and ten people, with the plush penthouse apartments even boasting roof terraces and hot tubs.

Dream Homes Budapest

Dream Homes Budapest is one of our top picks for self-catering, with stylish and spacious holiday apartments available right in the heart of the city. For larger groups, there’s a seven bedroom villa in Buda complete with indoor pool and epic cityscape views.

The best of Budapest

  • What’s the culture like
  • Eating
  • Drinking
  • Chilling
 

Whether you’re interested in military history or simply looking forward to getting the lads ruined in a ruin bar (pun definitely intended), Budapest is a pretty epic destination for a stag party.

While the previously mentioned Jewish Quarter should be top of your list for evening exploits, there’s also plenty to keep you entertained during the day. Head across the river to soak up Buda Castle, Fisherman’s Bastion and other Baroque masterpieces on the UNESCO World Heritage Castle Hill.

Back on the Pest side of the city, downtown Budapest offers up classy eateries and bars in addition to key heritage sites like St. Stephen’s Basilica and the Hungarian Parliament Building. For those keen to know more about the city’s WWII occupation, the aptly named House of Terror is a must-see.

 

Fancy trying some Hungarian goulash or kolbasz (spicy sausage)? Or maybe you have a few fussy eaters among your group? Thankfully, there’s ample choice when it comes to eating in Budapest, from traditional taverns to familiar chain restaurants.

Atmospheric Mazel Tov in trendy District Seven and family-run Rosenstein adjacent to Budapesht Keleti station are great spots for local dishes with a Jewish twist, while laidback Cafe Kor is as close as you’ll get to authentic Hungarian cuisine.

Keen to sample some street food? Head to the huge Downtown Market Hall on Hold Street where you can sample regional delicacies like juicy hog roast or wiener schnitzel.

 

Despite its reputation, the city’s nightlife isn’t all about Budapest strip clubs and palinka shots (although both are completely viable options). Just like many other Eastern European metropolises, partying in Budapest can be as cheap and cheerful or as sophisticated as you like.

The legendary ruin bars of the Jewish Quarter have undoubtedly transformed it into Budapest’s partying pinnacle. Highlights in the area include Szimpla Kert, with its warren of quirkily decorated rooms (picture graffitied walls, bizarre bric-a-brac and an old Trabant turned into seating), and Instant, a ramshackle club with no fewer than four dancefloors.

For something a little less rustic, you’re best off heading towards the river to somewhere like cool Corvin Club with its summer rooftop terrace. Budapest is additionally home to some great jazz clubs such as Opus and the Budapest Jazz Club - perfect for those who fancy jamming to some live music.

 

No stag do in Budapest is complete without having a soak in one of its world-famous thermal baths. The ideal place to nurse a hangover or indulge in an aroma massage for a bargain price, there’s nearly a dozen to choose from spread across the city.

The biggest and most popular is outdoor Széchenyi Baths in City Park, although smaller Gellért Bath - housed in a cool Art Deco building - and historic Rudas Baths - has traditional Turkish baths - are great, too.

Stag activities in Budapest

Searching for unique Budapest stag ideas? Take your pick of the following activities or get in touch if you’ve got something more specific in mind…
  • The Classics
  • Only in Budapest
  • Show-stoppers
  • Shoestring stags

Budapest bar crawl

A good, old fashioned bar crawl is made even better in Budapest thanks to cheap drinks and endless epic venues. Have a top night with the lads for as little as £15pp.

Bubble football

Like your typical kick around, but instead of football boots, shorts and a tee, you’ll be dressed in a hilarious bubble suit.

Wine tasting

Didn’t know Hungary had its own wine industry? Treat your tastebuds to some of the country’s best bottles with a tasting session led by an English-speaking sommelier.

Quad Biking

Head out of the city centre and speed through the stunning Hungarian countryside for a few hours on an adrenaline-rushing, quad bike adventure.

Budapest boat cruise

From sightseeing tours spotting major monuments to party boats complete with shots and dancers, Budapest cruises can be as cultural or as clubby as you like.

Széchenyi thermal baths

Book a morning spa session to help you all recover from your hangovers or head there at dusk for one of the bath’s insane pool parties.

Tank driving

Always wanted to get behind the wheel of a tank? Budapest stag dos offer the perfect opportunity to drive off-road in a Cold War-era machine.

KGB Shooting in Budapest

Let off some steam at a Budapest shooting range using high calibre firearms and receive technique tips from a weapons extraordinaire.

Sparty tickets

Combining a unique venue (i.e. one of the city’s incredible thermal baths) with shots and dancing, Budapest’s sparties are unforgettable.

Horse racing

It might not compare to Ascot, but Budapest’s Kincsem Park is still worth a look in. It’s the oldest racetrack in Hungary and a fun place for placing bets and guzzling cheap booze.

Private plane ride over the city

Want to wow the stags with an experience they’ll always remember? Try soaring across the city on a private plane tour.

Helicopter adventure

Similar to the above, this Budapest stag do activity sees you exploring the city’s key sights from up high in a helicopter.

Palinka tasting

Sip on various versions of Hungary’s national drink, a fruit brandy that’s capable of leaving you feeling worse for wear the following day.

Clamber up Castle Hill

The home of Buda Castle, rainbow-roofed Matthias Church and Fisherman’s Bastion, Castle Hill is a cultural hub and a great spot for some stag selfies.

Chill out on Margaret’s Island

From ice-skating in winter to segwaying in summer, Margaret Island is a sublime spot for outdoor pursuits. What’s more, it plays host to Sziget Festival in August, one of the biggest music festivals in Europe.

Stroll around the Great Market

Shop for souvenirs, snack on Hungarian specialties or taste some local booze at this truly immense market located in the heart of Budapest.
