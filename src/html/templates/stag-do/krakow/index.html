Kraków has a history that's fascinating but sombre, with areas that were once Jewish ghettos, death camps and Nazi strongholds. Today in Kazimierz, the Jewish quarter, you'll find the best kind of kosher restaurants, school kids in kippahs, brilliant bars, and a whole lot of artsy graffiti. Stare Miasto is on the other side of town. It's packed with traditional Polish restaurants, serving food so good it'll have you making weird awkward noises as you eat it.
From the buzzing Old Town to the beautiful Barbican to the hilariously named Planty Park, there's plenty of culture to tell your girlfriend/ mum/ nan about when you get home. Equally, between the vodka tasting joints that line Florianska Street to the strip cruises along the Vistula river, there's plenty of stag debauchery to be had in this Polish gem of a city.

The best of Krakow

The town is dotted with remnants of wartime history that still provide an important reference point for modern life. You'll find old warehouses that have been converted into ethereal cafes. You'll find that this city has transformed itself into a buzzy and trendy destination for modern art, bohemian bars, and the type of drool-inducing eats you'll have to see to believe.

 

You can find pretty much anything you like in this magnetic city, with a culinary scene that's so much more than its reputation of meat and potatoes. If you're hankering for some good authentic Polish fare, then head straight into the main square to Kiełbaski z Niebieskiej Nyski. Here you'll find two old boys in white wraparounds standing outside their iconic blue Nyska (a Soviet model van). They stand outside this food truck every evening from 6pm, grilling Kiełbasa (Polish sausages) on an open flame and granting salvation to the hungry, drunken masses. Get in line.

You'll be wanting to pop into Pod Temida for the pierogi alone. They serve homemade food, and the type of cheesy hearty dumplings that can rectify even the messiest night. If you really want to dive into the culture, try the traditional bigos; a hearty stew served piping hot and doused with tangy sauerkraut and cabbage.

For some Soviet-era appetisers, typically served cold with shots of vodka, it has to be Ambasada Śledzia. Tuck into huge portions of traditional herring in cream, followed by white sausage with horseradish. This popular joint gets busy early and stays open late but, with its endless array of vodka and beer options, it's a must-try for any stag do.

After that fifth round of shots last night, you desperately need to bring out the big guns. And that means pretty much anything on the menu at Corner Burger. Go for 'The Boss' – a 200g beef patty which comes with bacon, cheese, mushrooms, pickles and tomatoes. Then there's ‘Polish Pride', which comes slathered in local flavours like grilled blood sausage. If you fancy swapping your patty for steak, Moo Moo Steak & Burger Club is the only name you need to know.

You'll head to Gorące Paczki a number of time on your stag do. We'd put money on it. This place serves pączek, a traditional Polish doughnut typically filled with rose jam and glazed with sugar and candied orange peel. The sweet treats here are so beautifully executed, they'll have you feeling human again after just one bite.

 

In addition to the beer here that happens to be cheaper to buy than water, Krakow has a bar scene that's bursting at the seams.

First things first, head down to the slightly rusty and dusty Singer café, which gives a real sense of pre-war Krakow. The place is in the heart of the hipster district Kazimierz and was named after the Singer sewing machines, which have been transformed into tables. They serve every type of flavoured vodka you could ask for as well as very, very cheap beer.

Pijalnia Wodka i Piwa is an old-school bar influenced by Poland's socialist movement. You probably won't find doting waiters and fine dining but what you will find is dirt cheap drinks. We mean flavoured vodkas and beer for only £1. Yep, less than a packet of Hobnobs.

Cybermachina is a retro videogames dive bar where you can play games like Mario Kart on Nintendo, Xbox 360/One or PlayStation consoles. Seriously! Take the lads out for beers and old school gaming. What's not to love?

For vodka, it has to be Wódka Café Bar which is small but perfectly formed. Order their famous 6 shot vodka board for £4. It comes with all the classics like lemon and raspberry or spice things up with flavours of quince, ginger or chili chocolate.

 

Krakow isn't the place for chilling, but if you fancy seeing a bit of Polish history and culture on your trip, then Old Town is the place to go. Spend some time exploring the vast Main Market Square which is the focus of the Old Town, Europe's largest medieval town square. Check out the 16th-century Cloth Hall and the 15th-century Town Hall Tower. Have a mooch around the Wawel Royal Castle and then sit for a while at one of the many al fresco bars in the area and take in St Mary's Basilica. From here, head 15-minutes south and you'll find Kazimierz, once a separate town from Kraków but now one of the city's hippest areas.

Stag activities in Krakow

Searching for unique Krakow stag ideas? Take your pick of the following activities or get in touch if you've got something more specific in mind…
  • The Classics
  • Only in Krakow
  • Show-stoppers
  • Culture Vultures

Bar crawl

Nothing brings the lads together like a good old-fashioned bar crawl. Imagine having a VIP pass to all the places you want to go, unlimited drinks and every kind of drinking game? Leave it to us.

Vodka tasting

Discover Poland's national spirit as you stroll through Krakow's Old Town and, when we say stroll, we mean stumble because this stuff is strong.

Paintballing

Head to an outdoor Polish military training facility and prepare for all out carnage. If the weather's bad, opt for indoor paintballing and go head to head with your mates in an ex-storage facility.

Bubble football with transport

Like normal football but in a big inflatable bubble. You'll be falling out and crashing all over the show but it's all in the name of fun. Probably best leave the drinking until afterwards, though.

Kryspinów beach adventure

Kryspinów is a picturesque beach near Krakow, boasting white sand and Polish hotties. Whilst there, try wakeboarding sessions with a professional host and then get out on the water and try an SUP paddle board session. Epic.

Kalashnikov shooting

Spend your stag firing shots that would make Tony Montana proud. Thirty rounds on the gun that's fuelled a thousand revolutions, the Russian-made Kalashnikov AK47. Throw in some forfeits to raise the stakes.

Boat party with unlimited drinks

Watch the city of Krakow glide past you as you kick back with a beer on a 2-hour river cruise. There's also unlimited beer, gin, vodka and mixers, plus a sexy fire breathing show. If you party on Thursday or Saturday night, you'll get access to an afterparty pub crawl, free club entry plus welcome shots. Not too shabby.

Commie tour in Trabant

Visit Nowa Huta, Krakow's central socialist district in a super cool East German-made Trabant automobile. Your guide will keep you laughing 'til your sides hurt with stories from times gone by.

Polish beer tasting

Does what it says on the tin. Decide how long you fancy spending on the tour and then prepare to sample the masterpieces of Polish brewing. There might even be some local delicacies thrown in along the way like roast pork knuckle, which is traditionally eaten with a good beer.

Olympic white-water rafting

This popular stag activity offers the perfect opportunity to raise your pulse with a spot of white-water rafting. Is there a better hangover cure than blasting through freezing cold rapids? Depends who you ask… We think not.

4x4 off-roading

Check out the scenic beauty of Krakow's surrounding countryside. You'll get your very own instructor teaching you how to handle rugged terrain. After you've been trained with different driving techniques you can test drive by yourselves. Don't forget your driving license.

Sky diving

It's completely natural to be terrified of jumping out of a plane. It's also a once in a lifetime. Strapped to a professional instructor, you'll jump out 4000 metres above sea level. There's no thrill like it as you freefall towards Earth. Your parachute will then kick in and you'll glide to safe ground. Hopefully.

Zakopane tour with hot bath pools

It takes about an hour and a half to get from Krakow to the beautiful Zakopane. Here, you'll enjoy the best views from a cable car as you head up to Gubalowka. Check out the snow-capped mountains, take a dip in the hot bath pools and snap a few culture shots to show the missus back home.

Stroll the old town

The perfect start to exploring any new city is walking around its Old Town. Don't miss the Main Market Square, which is Europe's largest medieval town square.

Visit Wawel Cathedral

Explore Wawel Cathedral and the showpiece of Sigismund Chapel. Many regard it as the most beautiful Renaissance chapel north of the Alps. It's easily recognisable by its distinctive gilded dome.

St Mary's Basilica

Explore the altarpiece, the stained-glass windows, and the blue starred ceiling of St. Mary's Basilica. Walk around and soak up history.

Wieliczka Salt Mine

Visit the Wieliczka Salt Mine on a day trip from Krakow and discover a surreal underground world that was created hundreds of years ago. This is 135 metres underground and there are small spaces so if you're claustrophobic it's worth bearing that in mind.

Stag accommodation in Krakow

Hotel Alexander

Hotel Alexander offers bang for your buck. Rooms are spacious and come with the option to add a hearty Polish breakfast to the bill. You'll get free Wi-Fi, a safe, T.V and an in-room fridge as standard. You're also a short walk from Krakow's Old Town which offers historical sites, and plenty of buzzy bars and nightclubs

Hotel Atrium

This 3-star hotel in Krakow city centre offers cheap and cheerful room options. It's clean and friendly and situated walking distance from Planty Park. Pick from 113 guest rooms in the single, double, triple and apartment variety.

Puro Krakow

Rooms here are modern and sleek, with all the bells and whistles you need. The hotel features a suite option which comes with epic city views, a 40-inch Samsung LED TV, underfloor heating, soundproofing (handy) and fluffy bathrobes (whit woo). The location is the deal breaker – just a short hop skip and a jump away from Europe's largest square.

Sheraton Grand Krakow

Sheraton is a hotel chain that delivers time and again. Located right in the centre of town, this hotel offers spacious rooms and more facilities than you'll know what to do with. For those of you wanting a night a la The Hangover, book into The Royal Wawel Suite, with its 105 sq. of pure luxury. You get a master bedroom, a huge living area with a dining table for 8, a walk-in closet, kitchenette and shiny marble bathroom. Oh, you also get a separate shower area and epic Jacuzzi. No big deal.

Metropolitan Apartments

These cosy apartments, just a short walk from the Main Market Square, offer all you need if you want to go self-catering. The space and frills may be limited but the location more than makes up for it, right in the heart of Krakow's Old Town. Each apartment offers spacious sleeping areas, vintage décor and some even have a balcony. If you're wanting all the stags under one roof, then the 10-person apartment is just the ticket.

Old City Apartments

These stylish apartments sleep as many as 18 stags all in one snazzy penthouse. Opt for the rooms in the Main Square or Florinska if you want to be right in the heart of it. Oh, prices start from as little as 12 euros a night. Bit of a bargain.
