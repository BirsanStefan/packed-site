Franz Kafka’s lively hometown is full to the brim with hidden drinking dens, boozy boat parties and the sort of strip shows that’d make Hugh Hefner jealous. There are more microbreweries here than you can shake a pint at and, for those of you who want to take your stag to the next level, there’s the beer bikes, AK-47 experiences and even white-water rafting.

Prague’s Old Town is full to the brim with historic buildings and hip happenings, with hidden bars on every cobblestoned corner. When you’re wanting to sniff out the best places to eat, head to the Jewish Quarter or across the Charles bridge to Lesser Town where you’ll find every type of food you can imagine. If you’re looking for somewhere slightly less-touristy (and a little cheaper) then New Town is your go-to.

Whatever you choose to do, Prague has some of the best nightlife (and cheapest beer) in Europe, making it an ideal location to celebrate your best mate’s dying days of singledom.

Stag accommodation in Prague

Whether you’re wanting to splash the cash or do things on a shoe-string, you’ll find accommodation perfect for your group size and budget.

If you’re looking for a cheap crash pad, you’ll find plenty of clean, comfy hostels within stumbling distance of the city centre. On the other hand, if you’re looking for the sort of stay that’d make Jordan Belfort weak at the knees, we can sort that for you too.

If you’re wanting to bunk everyone under one roof, a spacious riverside city centre apartment might be an option? We know – so many decisions, so little time. Here’s a small selection of the great room options that we can lock down for your stag do in Prague.

Hostel Downtown

One of the most popular hostels in Prague, Hostel Downtown offers newly renovated rooms and some of the best views of Old Town. It’s located slap bang in the middle of the city – just a few minutes from Charles Bridge and Wenceslas Square..

Plus Prague Hostel

This cheap and cheerful homestay is a little further from town but offers large airy rooms, en-suite showers, a man cave, a salt-water pool and a year around sauna. Not too shabby, eh? You’re only 15-minutes from the city centre on the tram and have all the benefits of a spacious out of town hostel, with dorm rooms sleeping up to 8 people..

Atlantic Hotel

For a slightly snazzier option that’s still well within your price range, the centrally located Atlantic Hotel offers clean rooms and a sizeable hangover-cure breakfast. It’s located in Prague's historical centre, a short 10-minute walk from Old Town Square and few minutes from Namesti Republiky Square, with its wide array of pubs and buzzy bars..

Metropolitan Old Town Hotel

This swish hotel sits slap bang in the middle of Prague, on one of the city’s cobbled side streets. You can expect clean, airy rooms and 4 different restaurants. For those of you wanting to pool together into one space a la The Hangover, there are huge apartments with en-suite bathrooms that sleep up to 6 people.

NYX Hotel Prague

The NYX Hotel Prague makes for the ultimate luxury bachelor pad, complete with swanky rooms, funky decor and a classy hotel bar. If you can drag yourself out of the hotel, you’re a short stroll from the Old Town and uber trendy Jewish.

Aria Hotel Prague

Boasting a music library and the sort of flamboyant music-themed décor that would make J Gatsby jealous, Aria Hotel is a great option for those of you wanting to splash the cash. Stay in one of the vast bedrooms and benefit from private screening rooms, a curated music salon with an open fireplace, and exclusive access to the UNESCO Heritage Baroque garden Vybta.

Central Spot Apartments

If you’re only planning on using the room as a crash pad, you could do worse than these centrally located apartments. Central Spot Apartments sit on Krakovská Street, so you can stagger back from the city centre without getting lost. It has big windows, wooden floors and everything you need for a great stay.

Angel City Apartments

Angel City Apartments are a safe bet when it comes to self-catering bachelor pads. Treat yourself to a slick open plan apartment, complete with flat-screen TVs, a lounge area and a fully decked out kitchen. It’s also an excellent place to crash, being just 5-minutes from Prague’s main square.

The best of Prague

  • The best of Prague
  • What’s the culture like
  • Eating
  • Chilling
 

Prague is a city full of history. If you’re in the mood for a hungover mooch around the city then you’ve got Charles Bridge, the astronomical clock, and the pretty epic-looking Prague Castle. But, let’s face it, a stag party's never really been the time to hop aboard a tourist bus. Unless that tourist bus has a stripper pole, beer and bursting beats.

If you do want to soak up some culture, then start at Charles Bridge and head down to the Old Town along the cobbled streets. Here, you can have a traditional slosh-sesh in the Jewish Quarter but there’s also great hidden bars in Wenceslas Square, incredible hangover food, spontaneous street parties, and plenty of locals who will join your joyous revelry.

 

In addition to the beer here that happens to be cheaper to buy than water, Prague’s food scene is bursting at the seams. So, after a night out, if you’re not lying in bed feeling sorry for yourself or sweating out the booze in the hotel gym (yeah right) then get a load of these deliciously reviving food joints.

You’ll be wanting to pop into The Tavern more than once on your stag do. We’d put money on it. This place serves up greasy, satisfyingly salty fare like the loaded pulled pork cheddar fries and the southern fried chicken sandwich. This killer combo will have you feeling human again after just one bite.

After that fourth round of shots last night, you desperately need to bring out the big guns. And that means pretty much anything on the menu at Bohemia Bagel. Opt for the breakfast bagels which come with trimmings of bacon, eggs and copious amounts of cheese. There’s also biscuits and gravy, BLTs, huge cheeseburgers, and even an illustrious Philly cheese steak which works magic on throbbing headaches.

Wine O’clock, situated in the right next to the Apple museum, serves up what they claim to be the best meatballs in Prague. And we all know if there’s comfort food that can rectify even the messiest of nights, it’s meatballs. If you’re craving Mexican, it has to be Burrito Loco, whilst if you’re looking for some traditional Czech fare then look no further than u Houmra. The smažený sýr, or fried cheese balls, come highly recommended. Top it all off with a traditional chimney cake from Good Food Coffee & Bakery.

 

It’s a fact well known that Czechs know their way around a pint. The sudsy stuff here is so unbelievably cheap that you'd basically be losing money by not ordering one.

In fact, the Czechs have the highest consumption of beer per capita and, an evening at the ever-trendy Lokal, will explain why. You simply drink more when the fresh, cold Pilsner is this good and this cheap, especially when you don’t actually have to order it. The fine folks at Lokal just keep casually refilling your glass until you opt out. Yes, really.

Tucked away in a corner of the Jewish Quarter, you’ll find Hangar Bar a popular pseudo-speakeasy, chock full of waitresses dressed as air hostesses and old Pan Am airline décor. This basement bar is dimly-lit and fast-paced, with bartenders performing stunts for the crowd and waitresses keeping the drinks flowing long into the night.

If your crew are in the market for a solid beer bar, then Beer Geek is a must try. Here you’ll find 32 microbrews on tap, ranging from sours to stouts and everything in between. The chicken wings, which come in 11 different flavours, are notoriously delicious.

When you’re taking things a little slower, head down to The Pub. This self-service bar lets you serve your own pilsner from the beer taps at the centre of each table. You can log in and check how much beer you’ve knocked back. There’s also an outdoor terrace, which makes for a great place to clear your head.

 

Fancy something a little more chill? Hop aboard the historical tramway and look out for the city’s great landmarks. Get off at The Dubliner. Here you’ll find a welcoming little underground pub with everything you need to kick back with your mates and chill out. On tap, you’ve got Guinness, Heineken, and Staropramen, and there are two huge screens to watch the footy.

Stag activities in Prague

Searching for unique Prague stag ideas? Take your pick of the following activities or get in touch with us if you’ve got something a little more specific in mind…
  • The Classics
  • Only in Prague
  • Show-stoppers
  • Shoestring stags

Go Karting

A man of refined tastes, your soon-to-be-hitched pal knows exactly what he wants and sometimes that means a good ol’ game of Go Karting. Test out your driving skills, negotiate the hair-pin turns, and head for the chequered flag in this classic stag session.

Bubble football

Think normal football but in a big inflatable bubble. The zorb is firmly fastened to your body so you can't fall out and it means you’ll be crashing into each other to score goals and get your team in the lead. Much more fun than footy boots, shorts and a tee.

Beer bike

Fancy seeing the sights of the city by bike? Well, this one’s got shedloads of beer aboard and a sexy sober chauffer who’ll steer you around the city whilst you peddle and drink pints. What’s not to love?

Quad biking

Head out of the city centre and speed through the beautiful Prague countryside for a few hours of adrenaline-infused, quad bike adventure.

Indoor paintballing

Like regular paintballing but you can make this session unlimited with non-stop balls (paintballs, that is) and all-inclusive beers. Ah, bliss.

Private river cruise

Watch the city of Prague glide past you as you kick back with a beer on your very own private river cruise. This can be booked with or without a stripper, but we all know which option you’re going for.

Kalashnikov shoot

Spend your stag firing the type of shots that would make Tony Montana proud. Thirty rounds on the gun that's fuelled a thousand revolutions, the Russian-made Kalashnikov AK47. Throw in some forfeits to raise the stakes.

Staropramen brewery tour

Back in 1869, two broke bohemians dreamt of creating a perfect beer for the people of Prague. They succeeded. You can sample their masterpieces at the Staropramen brewery tour which lasts about 50 minutes and has you sampling four delicious beers.

White water rafting

This popular stag activity offers the perfect opportunity to raise your pulse with a spot of white-water rafting. Is there a better hangover cure than blasting through freezing cold rapids? We think not.

Tank driving

You’ll be picked up from your hotel and taken for a 30-minute driving session in a military tank. Oh, plus a round of beers. Epic.

Hover crafting

Like sentient robots and time-travel, if your best mate wants to spend his dying days of singledom zapping around on a hovercraft, who are you to tell him he can’t?

Military Jet Fighter Flight

Always wanted to fly your own fighter jet? Prague stag dos offer the perfect opportunity to fly high with a professional military pilot. Probably best done sober.

Indoor Sky Diving

It's completely natural to be terrified of jumping out of a plane. Ease yourself in with an indoor simulation. You’re still facing 160km to 280km winds but without the added risk of plummeting to your death.

Bungee jump

If the thrill seekers amongst you want an experience you won’t forget in a hurry, then bungee jumping over the Vltava River must be on your stag to-do-list.

Visit Prague Castle

This is a given. You can’t visit Prague without checking out the castle and mysterious Golden Lane. Do it for the ‘gram snaps at the least.

See the Astronomical Clock Strike

You’ll be elbow to elbow with a couple of hundred other tourists but did you even go to Prague if you don’t watch the clock strike an hour? Every hour this tower, which dates back to 1410, sets in motion an elaborate procession of the Twelve Apostles. Don’t leave without seeing it.

Stroll across the Charles Bridge

Walk along the historic Charles Bridge that crosses the Vltava river and look out at its many towers and statues. Enjoy the sounds of buskers as you walk across a piece of 14th-century history.

Eat beer-marinated Pork Knuckle

Pork knuckle marinated in a dark beer is one of the most famous dishes in Czech cuisine. Choose to have it with golden crispy potatoes or red slaw. Or both. There are no bad decisions here.

Scale Petrin Hill

Prague’s maze of cobbled streets and hidden gardens lead to the summit of Petřín hill where you can enjoy panoramic views across the city. Best bit? Absolutely free.
