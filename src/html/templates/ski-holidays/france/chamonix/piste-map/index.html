The Chamonix Mont-Blanc ski domain is made up of four large ski areas: Grand Montets, Brévent-Flégère, Balme Vallorcine and Les Houches. There are also several smaller ski areas spread across the valley including Savoy, Chosalots, Tourchet, Poya, Vormaine and Planards.

If you go for the Mont Blanc Unlimited ski pass, you can also access nearby ski resorts Courmayeur (Italy) and Verbier (Switzerland). You can find more information on which ski pass you need to access each of these areas below.

Which Chamonix ski pass do you need?

  • Chamonix le Pass
  • Mont Blanc Unlimited
 

Areas covered:

  • Brévent-Flégère
  • Grands Montets (except the top)
  • Balme - Le Tour - Vallorcine
  • La Vormaine, les Chosalets, Le Savoy, Plateau 2000 (Planpraz), Plateau de Lognan and les Planards

Suitable for beginners, intermediates and (if only for a few days) advanced skiers too. The pass covers some exceptional skiing between 1000 and 2675m in altitude and the only thing you won't be able to access is the top of the Grand Montets, the Aiguille du Midi, and Les Houches.

 

Areas covered:

  • Brévent-Flégère
  • Grands Montets (except the top)
  • Balme - Le Tour - Vallorcine
  • La Vormaine, les Chosalets, Le Savoy, Plateau 2000 (Planpraz), Plateau de Lognan and les Planards
  • Les Houches
  • Evasion Mont-Blanc ski area - Saint-Gervais or Megève (2-day pass minimum)
  • Aiguille du Midi, Montenvers - Mer de Glace and the Tramway du Mont-Blanc
  • Courmayeur (2-day pass minimum)
  • Verbier (6-day pass minimum)

The Mont Blanc Unlimited (MBU) pass covers everything Chamonix Le Pass does plus a huge range of extras. First, it gives you access to Les Houches which is a lovely ski area 6km down the road from Chamonix (accessed by shuttle bus) with long and picturesque descents lined by trees that is great for groups with mixed abilities.

The pass will also get you up the Aiguillle Du Midi cable car which goes right up to 3,842m and offers unrivalled, spectacular views across the alps. This is also where you go to ski the epic 20km+ Vallée Blanche ski run which is skiable by confident intermediates, although you do need a guide to navigate your route around the often very deep and dangeous crevasses dotted down the glacier.

Beyond that, any pass bought for 2 days or more will open access to Courmayeur in Italy, immediately on the other side of the Mont Blanc tunnel. A 6-day MBU pass means you can also ski in the exclusive Swiss resort of Verbier for a day. It's 65km away so it's worth taking a car.
