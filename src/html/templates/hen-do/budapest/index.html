Bestie about to get hitched? Treat her to a weekend she'll never forget and jet off to Budapest for a few days of affordable cocktails, calming spa sessions and charming European culture.
The Hungarian capital nestled on the majestic Danube River is packed with centuries of fascinating history, from Hapsburg palaces and Gothic cathedrals to poignant museums and memorials from its WWII occupation. Nicknamed the ‘city of baths', it's become a major hen do destination not just for its unique nightlife and stunning backdrops, but also its lush thermal spas. Locals have bathed in the city's soothing waters since Roman times and we're certain you'll find a relaxing dip the ideal hangover cure.
For more traditional hen do activities, Budapest doesn't disappoint, either. The city is a party mecca, with its iconic ruin bars providing a laid back location for hens to let loose. The best of Budapest

  • What’s the culture like

From its romantic architecture and brilliant ruin bars to its sensational spas perfect for some pre-wedding pampering, the Hungarian capital is heaven for hens.

Budapest hen dos offer the perfect balance of relaxation and adventure thanks to the cities unique mix of local traditions and contemporary culture. Party on the Danube, soak your weary limbs in mineral-rich waters or spend a few hours exploring endless magnificent monuments.

You'll find the city is divided by its world-famous river, with the Buda side being home to gorgeous heritage sites like Buda Castle, Fisherman's Bastion and the impressive Citadella on Gellert Hill. The small peak is also where you'll discover Gellert Spa, a spectacular spot if the group are keen to embrace the locals love of bathing (check online to see which days are mixed or woman only).

Staying in Pest? You'll find fantastic shopping opportunities along Vaci Street and the aptly named Fashion Street, plus quirky design stores in the Jewish Quarter and awesome souvenirs at the Central Market Hall - think Palinka (local fruit brandy), paprika and hand-painted porcelain.

Follow up your retail therapy with a drinks at a classy bar along the riverfront or an evening stroll to see the grand Hungarian Parliament Building beautifully lit up by the water.

 

Fancy trying some traditional Hungarian Goulash? Or maybe you'd rather stick to more familiar dishes at a popular chain? Whatever the hens are craving, you're bound to find something that satisfies all your stomachs in Budapest.

If you can't resist a food market, you'll be in your element at Budapest's Downtown Market Hall. The huge market sits in an impressive 19th century building on Hold Street and it's bursting with stalls and pop up restaurants serving up delicious local treats. Visiting around Christmas? Check out the festive food stalls outside St. Stephen's Basilica - we dare you to resist a piece of cinnamon and sugar coated chimney cake.

Budapest is additionally packed with charming cafes where you can grab a quick bite, from cosy eateries like Anna on Vaci Street for coffee and croissants to lush brunch spots with Insta-perfect pastel interiors like Vintage Garden in the Jewish Quarter. The district is also home to Mazel Tov, a popular Jewish-Hungarian restaurant with magical decor - think shimmering fairy lights, hanging plants and a menu of heart-warming dishes.

 

Budapest has a reputation for incredible nightlife. Shot local palinka in a ruin pub, get woozy on wine at a rooftop bar with river views or treat the bride to a sexy male stripper show - the sky's the limit!

If you're after cheap booze in a cosy setting, make a beeline for the shabby-chic ruin bars in the Jewish Quarter. Szimpla Kert is the largest and most visited of the ruin bars whose eclectic interiors (graffitied walls, neon lighting and a Trabant car turned into a seating area) is reason enough to visit.

For something a little more sophisticated, consider a champagne boat cruise along the Danube or a bar crawl in sophisticated District V. Check out the city's jazz music scene at cool bars like Opus or dance the night away at slick Corvin Club and urban Ötkert, both of which have amazing rooftop dancefloors.

The ultimate Budapest hen night out, though, has to be the sparty. It's essentially a party hosted at a thermal spa like the massive outdoor Széchenyi Baths. Picture people dancing, drinking and splashing around in their swimwear...

 

No hen do in Budapest is complete without having a soak in one of its world-famous thermal baths. The ideal place to nurse a hangover or indulge in an aroma massage for a bargain price, there’s nearly a dozen to choose from spread across the city.

The biggest and most popular is outdoor Széchenyi Baths in City Park, although smaller Gellért Bath - housed in a cool Art Deco building - and historic Rudas Baths - has traditional Turkish baths - are great, too.

Neighbourhoods not to be missed

Jewish Quarter

Found on the Pest side of the river, the Jewish Quarter (aka District Seven) was formerly a ghetto during WWII, but rose from the ashes to become one of the coolest neighbourhoods in Budapest. It's where you'll find the city's famed ruin bars - ramshackle yet welcoming drinking holes lodged inside abandoned old buildings - as well as dozens of cosy restaurants, independent shops and the beautiful Dohány Street Synagogue.

Castle Hill

No trip to Budapest is complete without strolling (or catching the funicular railway) up to the UNESCO World Heritage Castle Hill. Near its summit is where you'll spot grand Buda Castle - the home of the Hungarian National Gallery and former seat of the Hungarian royal family - while the top is scattered with stunning structures like dreamy Fisherman's Bastion and Matthias Church with its colourful roof.

Gellert Hill

Gellert Hill neighbours Castle Hill and it's a great spot for both bathing at the art deco Gellert Baths and taking in beautiful vistas. Increase your heart rate hiking up weaving pathways to the Citadella, a 19th century fortress that's now one of the best viewpoints in the city. If you're feeling a bit delicate, catch the number 27 bus instead.

Belváros-Lipótváros

More commonly known as District V, this is the very centre of Budapest and a real hub for shopping and dining. It's also where you'll discover key landmarks like St. Stephen's Basilica and the majestic Hungarian Parliament Building. The area's vast riverfront is home to the city's hippest bars and clubs, in addition to some of the best markets for treats and souvenirs.
