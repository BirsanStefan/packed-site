The best of Amsterdam

  • What's the culture like
  • Eating
  • Drinking
  • Getting around
 

This welcoming and highly historic city has long been known as a great place to let your hair down. As well as hundreds of bars and clubs perfect for a crazy girl’s night out, Amsterdam offers up opportunities and experiences to suit every kind of hen party.

Besides from its spectacular canals that make dreamy backdrops for any group photo ops, Amsterdam is jam-packed with incredible buildings. For a dose of history, book tickets for the Anne Frank House museum well in advance. Other hotspots include the huge RijksMuseum that’s home to a collection of enchanting dollhouses (you might recognise one of them from the BBC series The Miniaturist) and the Museum Van Loon, a restored 17th century canalside townhouse complete with romantic rose garden.

Art fans will also be in their element in Amsterdam. In addition to the impressive Van Gogh Museum, the innovative city is dotted with quirky creative spaces. Check out Stedelijk for contemporary exhibitions or go off the beaten track to Our Lord in the Attic - a pink church built in the attic of an old canal house which often hosts classical music concerts.

And if you’re keen to spend some time outdoors, spring and summer in Amsterdam call for walks around Vondelpark or day trips to leafy Amsterdam Bos. Alternatively, why not take a guided cycling tour around the picturesque waterways just beyond the city limits?

 

Not sure what Dutch cuisine entails beyond caramel waffles and mashed potatoes? Not to worry. Just like its incredible party scene, Amsterdam’s food offering is massively eclectic and caters to everybody’s tastes.

If you’ve planned your best gal’s Amsterdam hen do over the weekend, Sunday brunch at Bosco is a must. Nestled in the heart of the Oud West district, its mouth-watering menu is matched by cosy interiors, including a heated terrace.

For a locally loved lunch spot right by a canal, check out Buffet Van Odette that’s minutes from the Van Gogh Museum - don’t miss the fluffy omelettes with truffle cheese. Can’t resist a food market? Noordermarkt in Jordaan boasts stalls serving up hot crepes and melt-in-the-mouth apple pie.

On the final night of the hen do, why not treat the bride by booking a table somewhere extra special like the sleek two Michelin starred Daalder? De Kas overlooking Frankendael Park is also a stunning spot in the summer that boasts a unique farm to table ethos.

 

Whether you’re prosecco girls or you want to sip on a refreshing pint of Heineken, nights out in Amsterdam can be as laid back or as wild as you like.

De Wallen is not only home to the city’s racy Red Light District, but also some of the city’s liveliest nightlife venues. English-style bar The Tara offers a taste of home and a choice of five rooms to drink, dance and get merry in. For somewhere you can get all dressed up for, try one of the metropolis’s dance music clubs like Escape.

De Pijp is where you’ll discover a handful of brown bars (casual pubs), with wood-panelled T’Smalle being a popular spot right on the canal. If you’re keen to hop between bars, Gerard Doustraat is the best place to make a beeline for. Prefer a classy wine bar? Head to friendly Glou Glou for a glass and some tasty tapas-style bites.

 

As a city famed for its cycle-friendly streets, hiring bikes is by far one of the most popular ways to get around Amsterdam. You can hire bikes from rental spots across the city (typically outside stations), plus most hotels will offer good value rentals for the duration of your stay.

If the hens are stumbling back to your hotel after a few cocktails, you’ll be able to hail local cabs or use rideshare apps like Uber. Amsterdam also has a pretty decent public transport network which includes buses and trains, plus its flat terrain and stunning architecture makes it the ideal place for getting around on foot.

Hen activities in Amsterdam

Putting together an itinerary the bride will never forget is effortless in Amsterdam. Whether the group are keen to relax and soak up the sights or you all want to let your hair down with a truly unique outing, there’s no shortage of brilliant hen activities in Amsterdam. Here’s just a glimpse at what’s on offer.

  • The Classics
  • Only in Amsterdam
  • Show-stoppers
  • Shoestring hens

Cocktail class

Get tipsy off your favourite spirits while learning a new skill during a cocktail masterclass hosted by a hunky mixologist.

Prosecco bike tour

See the city’s top sights, stretch out your legs and sip on some chilled prosecco. What’s not to love?

Go Karting

Let off some pre-wedding steam with a competitive few hours racing around an indoor go kart track.

Amsterdam bar crawl

Head to a hip neighbourhood like De Pijp for a night out sipping on cocktails, mingling with locals and showing off your moves on the dancefloor.

Anne Frank House

Visit the former home of Anne Frank that’s been turned into a moving museum of her life, death and the city’s WWII history.

Guided bike tour

We may have mentioned Amsterdam cycling culture a few times already, however, it’s undeniable that a bike tour is the ultimate way to explore the Dutch capital!

Van Gogh Museum

Admire over 800 works of art by Vincent Van Gogh (a Dutch native) at this fascinating gallery in Amsterdam’s Museum Quarter just west of De Pijp.

Heineken brewery tour

Beer drinkers won’t want to pass up the chance to sample one of the world’s favourite lagers in its home country. Head to the Heineken Experience in De Pijp for tours and tastings.

Champagne canal cruise

A canal boat ride is a must when in Amsterdam. If you really want to rock the boat though, splash out on a champagne or dinner cruise.

Dance workshop

As a city that’s notorious for its sultry side, why not book the gals a sexy pole dancing lesson or a masterclass in burlesque?

Keukenhof tour

Tactically plan an Amsterdam hen do in May to make the most of the country’s gorgeous tulip blooms on a tour of Keukenhof Gardens 40 minutes south of the city.

Laze around in Vondelpark

Spend the day pootling around Amsterdam’s best-loved park where you can enjoy a picnic by the lake or a cup of coffee in the sunshine at De Vonteltuin.

Visit the Floating Flower Market

Holland is famed for its beautiful blooms. If you’ve not booked a hen do in Amsterdam during the spring bulb season, the Floating Flower Market is the next best thing.

Cycle along the canals

Rent bikes and go an adventure around Amsterdam’s waterways. Stick to the city centre or venture into the countryside during the warmer months to glide through pretty flower fields.

Shop on the Nine Little Streets

Keen to pick up some souvenirs? The Nine Little Streets in Jordaan are lined with cool boutiques and independent stores selling everything from jewellery to art prints.

Must-see neighbourhoods in Amsterdam

Jordaan

If you’ve got the Anne Frank House on your must-see list, this is the district to base yourselves near as the museum is right on its eastern edge. In addition to boasting picture-perfect canals criss-crossed with pretty bridges, Jordaan is a mecca for shopaholics thanks to dozens of indie boutiques in the Nine Little Streets and amazing antiques markets such as the Antiques Centre Amsterdam.

De Wallen

Amsterdam’s medieval heart is perfect for photo ops and also where you’ll discover buzzing nightlife spots nestled alongside centuries-old monuments. De Wallen is additionallly home to the neon-lit Red Light District, several sex museums that’ll definetely make you blush and numerous coffee shops which sell a little more than just espressoâ€¦

De Pijp

This cool and creative neighbourhood sits just south of the city centre and is home to some of Amsterdam’s best bars and shops. Unofficially known as the Latin Quarter, head there for an eclectic range of restaurants, lively markets like the popular Albert Cuypmarkt street market or to sip on Holland’s most famous alcoholic beverage at the Heineken Experience.

Hen accommodation in Amsterdam

Catching up on your beauty sleep after a full day of exploring and partying in Amsterdam is made simple thanks to the city’s litany of great accommodation options.

Strike a balance between comfort and cost at a trendy hostel, splash out on unrivalled luxury at a five-star retreat or find something ideal for your group in between. Gals who prefer the freedom that comes with self-catering will also find numerous apartment rentals lodged in cool heritage buildings and situated on the doorstep of Amsterdam’s top attractions.

Meininger Amsterdam Amstel

Handily located right next to Amsterdam Amstel station, this chic hotel not only has bundles of style, but also a purse-friendly price. Split the ladies between Meininger’s modern and comfortable triple rooms or bunk up together in a spacious six person dorm.

Generator Amsterdam

Generator hostels are renowned for their fun decor and sociable vibes - and Amsterdam’s newly opened branch is no exception. Nestled on the fringes of leafy Oosterpark inside a former zoological building, it has super trendy interiors (think lightwood and vibrant wall murals). Order up cocktails in the high-ceilinged lounge or seek out Generator Amsterdam’s hidden bar that’s rumoured to be located in the boiler room...

Apollo Hotel Amsterdam

Bed down for the night at this fancier option found on the edge of the De Pijp neighbourhood. As well as luxuriously designed bedrooms with all the modern amenities you’ll need to stay comfortable and connected, you’ll also be able to relax by the water with a glass of wine on the Apollo’s canalside terrace.

NH Amsterdam Centre

Housed inside an atmosphere red brick building, the NH Amsterdam Centre has been a hotel since the 1920s. It’s within walking distance of Vondelpark and is the perfect place for some R&R during a hen do in Amsterdam thanks to spacious suites and a plush wellness centre complete with a spa and sauna.

Hotel V Fizeaustraat

Those keen to splash out and treat the bride before her big day should consider this sleek design hotel just minutes from Amsterdam Amstel station. Snooze in cosy rooms with carefully chosen decor that wouldn’t look out of place in an interiors magazine and make the most of sophisticated shared facilities like the huge sun terrace.

W Amsterdam

Amsterdam’s branch of this upscale hotel chain certainly won’t disappoint gals after a little luxury. Located a short stroll from the Anne Frank House and inside an elegant former bank building, W Amsterdam not only offers luxe rooms with king size beds and beautiful ensuites, but also a spectacular heated rooftop pool and spa ideal for lazy mornings.

Hotel Boat & Co

Few things are more quintessentially Amsterdam than staying right next to a canal. Don’t think that sounds very glam? Hotel BOAT & CO in Houthaven will challenge your expectations with its swish one and two bed apartments. Wake up to uninterrupted waterfront views, enjoy easy access into the city by bus or bike and look forward to amazing onsite amenities like a chic restaurant, a spa and daily yoga classes on the deck.

Amsterdam City Center Apartments

Want the ease and freedom of an apartment, but still keen to stay in the heart of the city? Amsterdam City Center Apartments are located slap bang in the middle of De Wallen. As well as being surrounded by great bars, restaurants and other after dark attractions, the modern apartments come expertly well-equipped and can comfortably sleep up to six people.
