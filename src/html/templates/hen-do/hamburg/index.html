Whether your girls are beer drinkers or prefer a cheeky glass of prosecco, Hamburg is an epic European destination for a wild hen weekend away.
As well as glamorous nightclubs and relaxing beer gardens where you can sip on pints bigger than your head, this chic city in northern Germany has a reputation for being dynamic and multi-cultural. Dine out on every type of cuisine imaginable (not just currywurst), watch live music from across the globe or enjoy a sightseeing tour along the majestic River Elbe - the options are endless.
Hamburg’s more seductive side is easy to spot, too, in neighbourhoods like lively St. Pauli where unisex strip clubs sit alongside huge dance halls and sex shops bound to make the girls giggle. For something a little more tame, hens can hang out in Sternschanze where the hip streets are filled with Instagram-worthy cocktail bars and cool design boutiques.

The best of Hamburg

  • What’s the culture like
  • Eating
  • Drinking
  • Getting around
 

Hamburg’s friendly vibe and huge array of activity options makes it a top pick for hen weekends that are a little more out of the ordinary.

As well as a thriving party scene in the Reeperbahn district, it’s a city filled with wonderful eateries, fascinating cultural sites and some truly stunning green spaces for those days when you just want to stretch out on the grass in the sunshine.

Hens keen to learn more about Hamburg’s history and culture can stop off at numerous world-class museums, while the metropolis hosts numerous incredible festivals throughout the year, including the unmissable Christopher Street Day gay pride parade in early August.

Hamburg’s music scene is second to none. Reserve tickets to a gig at Kulturhaus 73 and Prinzenbar or follow in The Beatles footsteps with a night out at trendy Indra where the band regularly played in the 1960s before they made it big in the UK.

Beer is also undeniably a huge part of Hamburg’s local culture. If the girls are prone to glugging down the occasional pint, a tour of one of the city’s impressive breweries - followed by a few hours in a beer garden - should definitely be on the cards. Prefer bubbles? One of our top Hamburg hen do ideas is a dreamy prosecco bike tour...

 

Whatever your group has a craving for, you’ll definitely find it in Hamburg. The cosmopolitan city is blessed with a long list of excellent restaurants and cute cafes where you can pick up delicious food to suit every taste and budget.

Hens hoping to get a taste of Hamburg will want to put local favourite Grillstation on their foodie bucket lists as it’s thought to have the best currywurst and schnitzel in the country. Not a fan of fast food? Swap greasy packets for champagne flutes with a fancy feast at sleek Clouds that’s located in one of St. Pauli’s so-called Dancing Towers.

Can’t resist a pretty cafe? The city is teeming with them, from family-run Cafe Mimosa in Sternschanze that serves up delicious slices of cake and fresh coffee to yummy Sunday brunch in vintage-themed Cafe Brooks in sleepier Hasselbrook.

 

If you’re hoping to treat a bride-to-be to the ultimate girls’ night out, Hamburg won’t disappoint. Dance ’til dawn at clubs like Dollhouse and Moondoo along the Reeperbahn or head down to the river for a naughty striptease cruise.

If half-naked hunks and tequila shots aren’t quite your group’s scene, you’ll be better off in Sternschanze. Start your evening off relaxing in the shabby-chic interiors of Zoë Sofabar or taking advantage of the excellent happy hour at GoldFischGlas before making your way to Le Fonque for cheesy disco tunes.

Beer is the drink of choice for Hamburg’s locals and you’ll be able to enjoy a stein (that’s a large pint in the local lingo) surrounded by a buzzing crowd at one of two giant Hofbräu beer halls. Or check out one of the beach clubs along the shores of the River Elbe like Hamburg del Mar.

 

It’s not an overstatement to say Hamburg is sprawling. Thankfully, despite the city’s size, it hosts an amazing public transport system that makes getting from A to B super simple. Hop on an U-Bahn or S-Bahn train, catch a bus or cross the River Elbe on a ferry.

Hens staggering home, heels in hand, after a night out might want to hail a local taxi (the official ones are cream) or use a private car app like UberX. If you feel like being a little more active on a Hamburg hen weekend, consider hiring bright red StadtRad bikes from various spots around the city (including most train stations).

Hen activities in Hamburg

Wondering what to expect when it comes to hen do activities in Hamburg? From boozy bike tours through to lazy afternoons spent in fragrant, flower-filled parks, there are dozens of ways to make your group trip to this German party mecca a success. Here’s just a few suggestions:
  • The Classics
  • Only in Hamburg
  • Show-stoppers
  • Shoestring hens

Prosecco bikes

Combine sightseeing and pedalling with a few glasses of chilled prosecco on a drinking bike tour around Hamburg.

Beer tasting

Try some of the best beer in the city on a tasting tour at one of the city’s many popular breweries or a specialist craft beer bar.

Cocktail masterclass

Mix up a Mojito or learn the secrets behind the perfect White Russian at a cocktail making session in one of Hamburg’s trendy bars.

Quad biking

Girls need to let off some steam? Go off road on an exciting quad biking adventure in the countryside beyond Hamburg.

Spa day

Make your way to one of Hamburg’s top hotels for a few hours of primping and pampering in preparation for a big night out.

Prosecco party bus

Cruise around the city in a classic American school bus that’s been turned into a full-on party wagon (complete with fridges stocked with all the bubbly you’ll need to have a good time).

Visit the Gröninger brewery

If the bride has a taste for beer, she’ll love a guided tour of one of Hamburg’s oldest breweries. See the beer making process firsthand and taste some delicious beer straight from an oak barrel.

Go Electro Offroading

Strap yourselves into electric-powered chairs which you can take off-road and have fun racing around a muddy track.

Leap around at the Jump House

Unleash your inner child with a session at the Jump House, an impressive indoor trampolining centre in the heart of the city.

Waterskiing and wakeboarding

Test your skills and keep your balance (or risk falling into the chilly Elbe) with some amazing water sports.

RIB powerboat ride

Glide across Hamburg’s river at a 100km/h in a speedboat and look out for key sights along the shore with the help of a friendly local guide.

Burlesque dancing class

Hamburg is home to a handful of great cabaret and Burlesque clubs. Learn some moves you can then practice in the bedroom back home during a fun choreographed dance class.

Enjoy a river cruise

For something less likely to ruin your hair, set sail on a cruise along the Elbe. Choose from interesting sightseeing tours through to naughty night time cruises with a striptease for entertainment.

Explore Speicherstadt

Spend an afternoon roaming the city’s warehouse district that’s filled with cool shops and some of the best restaurants in Hamburg.

Take it easy at Planten un blomen

Get some fresh air at this gorgeous inner city park that’s also surrounded by some of Hamburg’s best shopping streets.

Shop at Hamburg Fischmarkt

Visit this huge historic market on the Elbe which is home to hundreds of stalls selling everything imaginable (not just fish).

Spend a day at StrandPauli Beach Club

If you’re planning a Hamburg hen do between April and October, StrandPauli Beach Club is the place to go to chill out with a few cocktails.

Keys areas to check out in Hamburg

St. Pauli

This central district is Hamburg’s party hub thanks in part to the lively Reeperbahn that’s thriving with after dark attractions. While it’s well-known with the lads for its strip clubs and sex shops, the area is also packed with fabulous bars and dance clubs. You’ll additionally find Beatles-Platz at one end of it - a square dedicated to the band who lived and performed in the city during the 1960s.

Sternschanze

Sternschanze is lodged just north west of the city centre and it’s Hamburg’s equivalent to London’s hip Shoreditch. You’ll spot huge graffitied buildings hosting numerous trendy bars where you can sip on expertly crafted cocktails or flaming shots of absinthe. The group will also be spoilt for choice by cosy streetside cafes serving up everything from classic German bites to delicious veggie food.

Hen accommodation in Hamburg

Five-star hotel? Pocket-friendly hostel? Whatever your group’s budget, you’re bound to find the perfect hen do accommodation in Hamburg.

Stay steps away from the city’s best bars, bed down right on the doorstep of some sensational shopping streets or venture a little further afield to relaxing riverside resorts with rooftop spas and pools.

Generator Hamburg

This popular hostel is right next door to Hauptbahnhof (Hamburg’s main station), making it a great base for visiting nearby sights or hopping on the S-Bahn to Sternschanze. Generator is also one of Hamburg’s largest hostels with stylishly decorated rooms (shared and private), plus a lively bar area with a gorgeous outdoor terrace.

Pyjama Park Hotel & St Pauli

Packed with quirky design features like bright wall murals and unique furnishings, Pyjama Park is a cut above your average city hostel. It’s also right on the lively Reeperbahn, meaning you won’t have too far to totter back after a night out.

City Hotel Monopol

Also located on Reeperbahn, City Hotel Monopol gives you the option of clean, spacious and ultra-modern ensuite rooms which are all - thankfully - soundproofed. It’s minutes from an S-Bahn station and surrounded by museums, from Sankt Pauli local history museum to the Erotic Art Museum that’s filled with hilarious artefacts…

ARCOTEL Onyx Hamburg

This is another St. Pauli pick, but you’ll find it at the far east end of Reeperbahn, making it a little less busy. ARCOTEL Onyx Hamburg’s contemporary rooms feature well-stocked mini bars and iPhone docking stations if you want to play some tunes or have a cheeky pre-drink while you get dressed up for a Hamburg hen night out. There’s also a serene spa and sauna ideal for when you’re all feeling a bit worse for wear.

Park Hyatt Hamburg

The Park Hyatt Hamburg is set in a more upmarket neighbourhood near the Main Station, but still has great transport links to the city’s top nightlife areas. The hotel’s plush features reflect its five-star ranking, from spacious suites with soft beds and top-of-the-range TVs to complimentary access to its luxury spa and fitness centre.

The Fontenay

Lodged along the shores of Alster Lake and close to the trendy Jungfernstieg shopping district, The Fontenay is definitely for hens after some luxury. The more expensive suites come with king size beds and beautiful balconies, while the hotel’s top floor is home to a spectacular rooftop pool and spa.
