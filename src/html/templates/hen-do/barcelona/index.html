She’s engaged! Finally! You know what that means: double-tap that ring pic and start planning the bachelorette. What better place to celebrate your best gal pal’s last few nights of singledom than in beautiful Barcelona?
A city so good Freddie Mercury wrote a song about it. Barcelona is known for its vivacious nightlife, its melting pot of amazing cuisine, and its world-class beaches. It also boasts some of the most iconic architecture in Europe and glamorous nightclubs perfect for dancing and romancing.
From flamenco sessions to paella lessons to ice bars on a beach, you’ll never be short of things to see and do in this European city of culture.

The best of Barcelona

  • What's the culture like
  • Eating

With its iconic architecture, great nightlife and a laid-back vibe, Barcelona’s basically a dream come true -- provided your dreams are filled with copious amounts of sangria, delicious food, and plenty of Mediterranean hunks.

Soak up a buzzy centre filled with bars and delicious restaurants. Around the outskirts of the city you’ll find the beautiful Parc Guell, one of Catalan architect Antoni Gaudi’s most famous creations. From here gaze out towards the sparkling Med and at Gaudi’s second famous undertaking, the Sagrada Familia cathedral.

Back in the centre you can’t help but lose yourself in the live music scene: the fiery rhythms of flamenco, sultry jazz spilling out of basements, and hands-in-the-air Catalan beats. Spend nights sipping sangria whilst a sexy Latin lover teaches you how to make the perfect paella.

 

More than the history, the music, or even the people, a visit to Barcelona is defined by one thing: Catalan cuisine. But between all the top-notch bolt holes slinging tapas, Spanish ham, and dreamy crema Catalana desserts, it’s easy to feel overwhelmed. Let us break it down…

If you’ve got a penchant for paella, then head to Barraca. The food here is homemade and there’s seating outside so you can tuck into your three courses with a side of sunset. Indulge in the tasty and creamy homemade burrata if it’s on the menu.

If the girls fancy something hand-held, super-sweet and always satisfying then head on down to Lukumas for the kind of doughnuts that’ll ruin your life. These tiny mouthfuls of pleasure will be on your mind long after you’ve left Barcelona. You’ll wake up in the middle of the night craving them and nothing else will do. Nevertheless, get your handheld fix of pleasure whilst you can.

You’ll be wanting to pop into Bitácora for the tapas alone. They serve homemade fare, and the type of patatas bravas that’ll have you smiling all night – piping hot and doused in a delicious garlicky sauce.

La Paradeta is usually packed with young locals and travellers. Dig into fresh grilled prawns, a cod pie that will make you weep, or a fisherman’s catch straight from the sea.

For pizza, it has to be Il Piccolo Focone where you’ll find big bold flavours and pizzas the size of Magic Mike’s biceps. Sink your teeth into a layer of fragrant tomato sauce bubbling atop ropy cheese and thick, doughy crispy crust. Is there a better way to end a night? We think not.

Be Chocolat, tucked down a cobbled street that’s been around since the Middle Ages, serves up the type of delicious chocolatey goodness that’ll cure a hangover with just one taste. All of the confections are handcrafted on-site in this minute chocolate shop. Explore the city’s Gothic Quarter while sucking on a wafer-thin slice of salted caramel or munching a dark chocolate melt in the mouth truffle. Grab a bag of churros to go too because, you know, YOLO.

 

Each of Barcelona’s neighbourhoods boast such an immense drinks offering that deciding where to go can be a daunting task. We’re here to help…

There are good cocktails and then there are the cocktails served up by Dr. Stravinsky which are damn near perfect. The place is sexy and dimply lit, with an array of devilishly good-looking bartenders, ready to whisk you off your feet with their flavoured liquors.

Head into the L’Antiga Esquerra de l’Eixample neighbourhood and you’ll find Solange. Holy Smokes this place is good. It’s the kind of place you’d expect to meet James Bond and you might – his name will probably be something a little more exotic though. He’d hand you one of Solange’s signature cocktails as you got comfy at the stately wooden bar.

Hit La Ramblas next, which is chock-full of great bars and, once you’ve made your way to the very end of the boulevard, you’ll find the neighbourhood of Raval. One name for you here: Magic Club. This place is friendly. It’s always rammed. It’s always awesome. 80’s pop and disco and two huge dance floors that hold up to 1000 revellers.

If you and the girls fancy starting the night with something a little more sophisticated, you won’t want to miss a visit to Monvínic, a wine bar that’s made quite a name for itself recently. Pick from some of the most sumptuous tapas dishes and over 3,000 varieties of wine. Cin cin.

The Gata Mala bar, with its warm atmosphere and free flowing drinks is one of the best-kept secrets in the Gràcia neighbourhood. Friendly prices, free tapas with every beverage and the sort of G&Ts you could swim in. Hell yes.

 

Chillers and down‐time seekers can hit up the dazzling beaches ‐ with dazzling bodies to match ‐ which line this coastal city. The stretch of sand around Port Olímpic Marina is a great spot for people‐watching and a few, languid hair‐of‐the‐dogs.

Barcelona’s beaches also play host to day‐time parties and raves for if and when you feel like switching up gears. But if you prefer to blaze your own trail and keep a distance from other stag groups, there are secret spots that Packed can guide you to. More on that below.

Hen activities in Barcelona

Searching for unique Barcelona hen ideas? Take your pick of the following activities or get in touch if you’ve got something more specific in mind…

  • The Classics
  • Only in Barcelona
  • Show-stoppers
  • Shoestring hens

Cocktail making

Learn how to muddle masterfully as you knock back drinks at this cocktail class. Your very own expert bartender will tutor you through how to make the perfect drink – Pornstar Martini, anyone?

Beer bike

Fancy seeing the sights of the city by bike? Well, this one’s got plenty of booze aboard and a sexy sober chauffer who’ll steer you around the city whilst you peddle and party.

Go Karting

when you fancy taking a step away from the bars, try your hand at a little go karting fun. Test out your driving skills, negotiate the hair-pin turns – and the loser gets in the drinks later.

Boat/Catamaran cruise

From relaxing beach tours with a BBQ to party boats complete with free-flowing booze, Barcelona cruises can be as cultural or as clubby as you like.

Ice Bar

Fancy sipping cocktails at the world’s only ice bar on a beach? ’Course you do. Sup from an ice cup at around -5ºC where you can chill out with the girls for up to 45 minutes – depending how you handle the cold.

Private yacht hire

Oh, how very Sex in the City of you. Head out onto the sea with strong drinks and your best gal pals. What could be better?

Paddle boarding

This popular hen activity offers the perfect opportunity to reconnect with nature and try something fun outdoors. It’s also a great way to slip into a bikini and top up those pre-wedding tans.

Flamenco or Salsa classes

Where better to try out your sizzling salsa and fiery flamenco moves than in Spain? If the bride-to-be loves to dance, this is a no brainer.

Flyboard experience

If your gal pal wants to spend her dying days of singledom zapping around on a hovercraft, who are you to tell her she can’t?

Waterpark excursion

Port Aventura is most famous theme park in Catalunya. Located in the south of Barcelona in the city of Salou, Tarragona, it attracts around 5 million visitors a year and once you’ve tried the water rides, you’ll see why.

Helicopter ride

Want to wow the hens with an experience they’ll always remember? Try soaring across the city on a helicopter tour.

Kayak or snorkelling tour

Head out into Costa Brava’s crystal-clear waters, paddle in the warm Mediterranean Sea and swim out to explore hidden caves and beautiful underwater marine life.

Hot air balloon ride

Explore the city’s key sights from up high in a hot air balloon. The sunrise (good luck with your sore heads) or sunset tours are the most atmospheric.

Paella cooking class

Spend a night sipping sangria whilst a sexy Latin lover teaches you how to make the perfect paella.

Tapas tour

What better way to discover the true taste of Spain than learning about local food and culture in a tasty hands-on tapas tour?

Tibidabo and Montjuïc

Montjuic Mountain is a tourist magnet and you’ll soon see why. Head up in a cable car and take in the soaring views. Tibidabo is magical at sunset.

Park Güell

For sunset strolls you can’t beat a trip to the park that is one of Catalan architect Antoni Gaudi’s most famous creations. From here gaze out towards the sparkling Med, and at Gaudi’s second famous undertaking, the Sagrada Familia cathedral.

The beach

There are plenty of beaches to choose from just a short stroll outside of the city. Barceloneta is one of the most popular and perfect for sipping an iced margherita and topping up that tan.

Cultural sights

Shop for souvenirs, snack on Spanish specialties and soak up the culture as you stroll through this beautiful city.

Hen accommodation in Barcelona

Whether you’re wanting to splash the cash or do things on a shoe-string, you’ll find accommodation perfect for your group size and budget.

If you’re looking for a cheap crash pad, you’ll find plenty of quirky hostels that are clean, comfy and within stumbling distance of your bars and restaurants. On the other hand, if you’re looking for the sort of stay that’d make Carrie Bradshaw jealous, we can sort that for you too.

Those of you who want to bunk everyone under one roof, a spacious city centre apartment might be a good option. We know – so many decisions, so little time. Here’s a small selection of the great room options that we can lock down for your hen do in Barcelona.

Here's a slice of what's on offer to give you an idea:

  • A little extra luxury

TOC Hostel

If a swish place to rest your head is what you’re after, then look no further than TOC Hostel. It’s set in the heart of Barcelona, just 750 yards from Plaza Catalunya Square, and offers an outdoor pool and a stylish terrace. Rooms come with air conditioning, heating, flat-screen TVs and many have a private bathroom. There’s a shared TV area where you can catch up on Sex in the City, as well as a kitchen and lounge area for when you just want to chill out with a vino.

Generator Hostel

Generator Barcelona is located right next to the fashionable Passeig de Gracia Avenue. The designer hostel offers a 24-hour reception and girl-only dorms, which is handy. You can also book a family room which sleeps 6. If you really want to take things up a notch, book into the penthouse apartment – which comes with panoramic views across the city.

H10 Madison

For a slightly more upmarket option that’s still well within your price range, the stunning M10 Madison is a brilliant option. Located in the heart of Barcelona, just a few metres from the cathedral, this swish 4-star hotel is housed in an entirely restored early 20th-century building. Rooms are huge, with comfortable beds and all the trimmings. On the top floor you’ll find an amazing rooftop plunge pool which looks directly out onto the cathedral. Whit woo.

NH Collection Barcelona Podium

Decorated in swathes of brown and cream, with elegant hardwood floors, you’d be forgiven for thinking you’d stepped into the pages of a homeware magazine. The junior suites have a separate living room with a sofa bed and a flat screen TV, whilst the triple rooms are great if you want your gals all in one place – extra beds can be added at no extra cost. Oh, there’s also a skyline pool, roof terrace and cocktail bar on the top floor of the hotel. No big deal.

W Hotel

If you want to take things up a notch, then W Hotel Barcelona is the place to do it. This hotel offers luxury beachfront accommodation with rooms that boast panoramic views across the Mediterranean Sea. There’s also a sauna, a swimming pool, a rooftop terrace and a first-rate restaurant which serves a glamorous Sunday brunch.

Grand Central Hotel

If you looked up the word ’first class’ in a dictionary, you’d find a photo of this hotel. It has been elegantly refurbished and each room is unique in its décor and style. The bathrooms here boast rain-effect showers and luxury toiletries. Opt to stay in one of its deluxe suites like the Junior Suite, which sleeps 3.

Midtown Apartments Barcelona

When you fancy having all the girls together, a 3-bed 2-bath apartment in the centre of Barcelona makes for a great option. Midtown Apartments are a short 6-minute walk from both Paseo de Gracia and central Plaza Catalunya and offer stylish modern rooms with a seasonal pool and solarium on the rooftop.

Paseo de Gràcia Bas Apartments Barcelona

This is one of our top picks for self-catering, with stylish and spacious apartments available right in the heart of the city. You have bars and restaurants on your doorstep as well as nifty features like a Smart TV (Magic Mike on Netflix, anyone?) and hydro-massage shower.
