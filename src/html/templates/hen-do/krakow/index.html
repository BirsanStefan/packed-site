Krakow is one of the most appropriate (and acceptable) places in the world to go boozy brunching, dancing 'til dawn and sipping wine until the wee hours of the morning. Not only is the city brimming with bistros serving all-day breakfasts, killer cocktails and art galleries you could spend hours in, it's also incredibly cheap. A shot of vodka here actually costs less than a packet of Hobnobs. Yes, really.
Spend afternoons wandering through Pauza's modern art galleries and evenings throwing shapes in its quirky basement nightclubs. Sip cocktails, sink shots, dance with sexy strangers and then stumble through Krakow's cobbled streets back to your hotel via a Kosher bakery.
The town's chequered wartime history will draw you in, but it's the buzzy Bohemian bars and all-night party vibe that'll make you never want to leave.

The best of Krakow

Krakow is a walker's city. There's so much wartime history engrained in the streets and the architecture. You'll find old warehouses that have been converted into glowing ethereal cafes. You'll find cocktail bars that are open all night. You'll find brightly-lit drinking dens serving every possible type of vodka and a vibe that's perfect for a last-hurrah bachelorette extravaganza.

 

Trying to decide on a dining location in Krakow can be really difficult… especially when you're plagued with hunger, a hangover... and so many amazing foodie options to choose from. Fortunately, we're here to save you from dying indecision. If you're taking the girls out for some good authentic Polish fare, then head straight to Zalewajka. Here you'll find deliciousness like wild boar goulash and traditional Polish potato cakes. It tends to get busy in the evening so either book ahead or get in line.

Head to Pod Temida for the pierogi alone. Pierogi is a traditional type of cheesy, hearty dumpling and it's hard to eat just one. For a cosy homemade Polish meal, with killer cocktails and candlelit tables it has to be Starka.

For some serious fine dining, sure to impress all the bachelorettes, head straight to Wierzynek. Tuck into beautifully put together dishes like foie gras, followed by perfectly cooked melt-in-the mouth beef tenderloin. Opt for the honey-caramelised porcini mushroom ice cream to finish. It may sound odd, but people love it.

If you and the girls fancy a night on the vino, start it at Pierwszy Stopien and, for heaven's sake, order the cheese and charcuterie board. It comes stacked to the top with regional cheeses from Lomnica, Krakowska, Lisiecka sausage, homemade goose pâté, homemade red onion jam, tangy pickles, crusty bread and salted butter. Drooling yet?

For those of you who fancy something sugary, there's only one name you need to know: Chimney Cake Bakery. This place serves ridiculously buttery and all-around delicious sweet treats. Think perfectly crisp dough, baked on an open fire, and then coated in cinnamon, cocoa powder and sugar. Not just that – these bad boys come in all sorts of flavours. Think vanilla glazed, chocolate sprinkled or stuffed with flavoured ice-cream, peanut butter and gooey brownie pieces. Jesus.

 

In addition to the beer here that happens to be cheaper to buy than water, Krakow has a bar scene that's bursting at the seams.

First things first, head down to the slightly rusty and dusty Singer café, which gives a real sense of pre-war Krakow. The place is in the heart of the hipster district Kazimierz and was named after the Singer sewing machines, which have been transformed into tables. They serve every type of flavoured vodka you could ask for as well as very, very cheap beer.

Pijalnia Wodka i Piwa is an old-school bar influenced by Poland's socialist movement. You probably won't find doting waiters and fine dining but what you will find is dirt cheap drinks. We mean flavoured vodkas and beer for only £1. Yep, less than a packet of Hobnobs.

Cybermachina is a retro videogames dive bar where you can play games like Mario Kart on Nintendo, Xbox 360/One or PlayStation consoles. Seriously! Take the lads out for beers and old school gaming. What's not to love?

For vodka, it has to be Wódka Café Bar which is small but perfectly formed. Order their famous 6 shot vodka board for £4. It comes with all the classics like lemon and raspberry or spice things up with flavours of quince, ginger or chili chocolate.

 

Krakow isn't the place for chilling, but if you fancy seeing a bit of Polish history and culture on your trip, then Old Town is the place to go. Spend some time exploring the vast Main Market Square which is the focus of the Old Town, Europe's largest medieval town square. Check out the 16th-century Cloth Hall and the 15th-century Town Hall Tower. Have a mooch around the Wawel Royal Castle and then sit for a while at one of the many al fresco bars in the area and take in St Mary's Basilica. From here, head 15-minutes south and you'll find Kazimierz, once a separate town from Kraków but now one of the city's hippest areas.

Hen activities in Krakow

Searching for unique Krakow hen ideas? Take your pick of the following activities or get in touch if you've got something more specific in mind…

Bar crawl

Nothing brings the girls together like sharing stories as you stumble from bar to bar, eh? Now imagine having a VIP pass to all the places you want to go, unlimited drinks and every kind of drinking game? We can help with all that – the fact you decided to wear 6-inch heels to walk through town, not so much.

Chocolate making classes

Chocolate has the ability to make people very happy. Book yourself in for a workshop to learn about the art of chocolate making and how to get the perfect flavour.

Luxury spa

When your feet are swollen, and another night of drinking is starting to make you feel nauseous, it's time to whip out the good ol' spa trip. Krakow is brimming with luxurious spas where you can book massages, facials and all sorts of other sweet-smelling holistic therapies.

Go karting

When you fancy taking a step away from the bars, try your hand at a little go karting fun. Test out your driving skills– and have the loser get in the drinks later.

Cocktail making

Learn how to muddle masterfully as your sexy expert bartender shows you the ropes. Afterwards you can sit back and sip a tropical cocktail as you toast to the fruits of your labour.

Kryspinów beach adventure

Kryspinów is a picturesque beach near Krakow, boasting white sand and plenty of Polish hunks. Whilst there, try wake boarding sessions with a professional host or top up those pre-wedding tans with a spot of sunbathing.

Kalashnikov shooting

Spend your hen firing shots at all those exes who did you wrong. We're kidding of course, but you can have a go at perfecting your shoot and aim and blowing through ammo on a Kalashnikov range.

Boat party with unlimited drinks

Watch the city of Krakow glide past as you relax with a cocktail (or 3) on a 2-hour river cruise. There's also unlimited beer, gin, vodka and mixers – plus a sexy fire breathing show.

Commie tour in Trabant

Visit Nowa Huta, Krakow's central socialist district. Hop into a quirky East German-made Trabant automobile. Your guide will keep you laughing as you tour Krakow's historic and architectural gems.

Vodka tasting

Discover Poland's national spirit as you stroll through Krakow's Old Town and, when we say stroll, we mean stagger because this vodka's strong.

Olympic white-water rafting

This popular hen activity offers the perfect opportunity to get your pulse racing with a spot of white-water rafting. Sure to cure any hangover with just one session.

4x4 off-roading

Check out the scenic beauty of Krakow's surrounding countryside. You'll get your very own hunky instructor teaching you how to handle rugged terrain. After you've been trained with different driving techniques you can test drive by yourselves.

Sky diving

This is an experience you and the girls will never forget. Strapped to a hunky professional, you'll jump out 4000 metres above sea level. There's no thrill like it as you freefall towards Earth. Your parachute will then kick in and you'll glide onto safe ground. Hopefully.

Zakopane tour with hot bath pools

It takes about an hour and a half to get from Krakow to the beautiful Zakopane. Enjoy the best snowy views from a cable car as you head up to Gubałówka. Take a dip in the hot bath pools and snap a few shots for the 'gram. #selfie

Stroll the old town

The Old Town is one of the must-see places to visit in Krakow – it really gives you a sense of the city in times gone by. Don't miss the Main Market Square, which is Europe's largest medieval town square.

Visit Wawel Cathedral

Explore Wawel Cathedral and the showpiece of Sigismund Chapel. It's easily recognisable by its distinctive gilded dome.

St Mary's Basilica

Explore the altarpiece, the stained-glass windows, and the blue starred ceiling of St. Mary's Basilica.

Wieliczka Salt Mine

Visit the Wieliczka Salt Mine on a day trip from Krakow and discover a surreal underground world that was created hundreds of years ago.

Schindler's Factory

Any history buff will appreciate a trip to Oscar Schindler's factory. Here you can visit Schindler's preserved office and learn more about his incredible story.

It's hard to get more central than Hotel Alexander. Rooms here are gorgeously spacious and come with the option to add a hearty Polish breakfast to the bill. You'll get free Wi-Fi, a safe, T.V and an in-room fridge as standard. You're also a short walk from Krakow's Old Town, with its plethora of buzzy bars and nightclubs.

Hotel Atrium

The 3-star hotel Hotel Atrium in Krakow offers plenty of bang for your buck. It's clean and friendly and situated walking distance from Planty Park (great name for a park, that). Pick from 113 elegant guestrooms and enjoy a cheeky hotel discount at the nearby Dermed spa centre.

Puro Krakow

Rooms here are sexy and sleek, with all the bells and whistles you need. No big deal.

Metropolitan Apartments

These cosy apartments, just a short walk from the Main Market Square, offer all you need when you're going self-catering. These stylish apartments sleep as many as 18 people all in one snazzy penthouse. Opt for the rooms in the Main Square or Florinska if you want to be right in the heart of the action. Prices start from as little as 12 euros a night. Bit of a bargain.
