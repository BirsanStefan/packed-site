As an historic capital city with plenty of modern conveniences, Madrid hen dos really do have it all when it comes to balancing culture with boozy antics.

While Madrid's centre may be packed to the rafters with gorgeous heritage sites (the Royal Palace is a must-see) and galleries showcasing jaw dropping masterpieces, there's plenty of culture to soak up in farther-flung neighbourhoods, too.

Foodie fanatics and gals who love a glass of wine will be spoilt for choice by traditional tapas bars, food markets, flamenco clubs and vinotecas (wine bars) in vibrant barrios such as La Latina, Chuecha and Chamberi. Make a beeline for colourful Malasa√±a (the birthplace of the city's Movida Madrile√±a cultural movement in the 1980s) if the bride's guilty pleasure is anything vintage.

If you're keen to stay sober for at least a few hours of a Madrid hen weekend, consider checking out the city's Golden Triangle of Art - the Prado, Reina Sofis and Thyssen-Bornemisza museums - or treating yourselves to some new clothes with a shopping spree along Calle Princesa.

Spain's Moorish history means there's plenty of soothing spas to visit too, including the dreamy Al √Åndalus Hamman baths. Prefer a relaxing walk around a pretty park? Check out local favourite Parque del Retiro or soak up panoramic cityscapes at Cuartel de la Montana Park.

 

You can't visit Madrid without tucking into some tapas, whether that's traditional dishes like jamon, croquetas and patatas bravas or options tailored for vegetarians and vegans. Calle de la Cava Baja in the La Latina neighbourhood is well-known for its range of tapas bars, but you'll also find plenty of other options to get your fill in across the city.

Near the Royal Palace is where you'll spot Mercado de San Miguel, one of the oldest covered markets in Madrid that's packed with stalls serving up all sorts of mouthwatering morsels. Eco-friendly Mama Campo on Plaza de Olivade is also a great pick for delicious Spanish classics made from organic, locally sourced ingredients, while Casa Dani in the chic Salamanca barrio is famed for its tortilla de patatas (Spanish omelette).

Can't resist a steaming plate of freshly-made paella? Centro is bursting with restaurants serving up this Valencia-born dish. For a range of different types (including a tasty seafood version made with squid ink rice), book a table at La Paella de la Reine which is widely thought of as the best paella place in Madrid.

If you're after a wide range of eateries to suit the entire group's taste buds, you'll be swamped by the city's excellent selection of global cuisine options. Sample sensational Spanish-Japanese fusion food at Yakitoro by Chicote in Chueca or sit down to a classic American brunch at The Toast Cafe in Chamberi.

 

Drinking will likely be a prime part of a Madrid hen do. Like a lot of Spanish cities though, everything seems to happen a little later in Madrid - and that includes going out partying. Expect to be dancing and drinking into the early hours, whether you go for a glass (or three) of Cava at an atmospheric vinoteca or shots and dancing at a buzzing nightclub.

The streets surrounding Puerta del Sol are lined with awesome afterdark venues where hens can let their hair down, from buzzing Joy Eslava with its huge dancefloor to El Sol for live rock and pop bands. Off the beaten track, you'll discover more intimate drinking holes and casual disco-pubs in Chueca or you could wander down to La Latina to watch a fiery flamenco show at Corral de la Morer√≠a.

In the warmer months, there's nothing better than visiting one of Madrid's many rooftop bars. Sip on well-made mojitos as you watch the sunset at cosy El Viajero in La Latina, relax poolside at the Gymage Lounge Resort terrace or enjoy some sangria before a music or theatre performance on the roof of the Circulo de Bellas Artes.

 

Worried about navigating a brand new city? You needn't be in Madrid. The Spanish capital has an amazing public transport system, plus you can use the same tickets on buses and the metro system. Alternatively, there's the eco-friendly BiciMAD scheme which lets you hire bikes for as long as you need them from over 120 spots around the city.

If you're staying in the Centro area, you'll likely find most attractions are within walking distance. Planning a few night's out during an upcoming hen do in Madrid? Forget staggering home, heels in hands; the city has thousands of affordable taxis you can hail down or book in advance. Official ones are white with a red stripe on the passenger door and you can pay for them with cash or credit card.

Hen activities in Madrid

While Madrid's historic, cultural and culinary charms could keep you occupied for days on end, you'll want to pull out all the stops for a bride's final weekend of singledom. Whatever kind of activity you think she'll love, here are just a few Madrid hen do ideas to get you started:

Madrid rooftop bar crawl

Madrid is blessed with numerous rooftop bars which make the most of the balmy summer evenings. Enjoy hopping from roof to roof on a lively alfresco bar crawl.

Relaxing spa day

Treat your Madrid hen party to some much-needed pampering with a lush spa day at one of the city's top wellness venues.

Tapas and wine tasting

It doesn't get more Spanish than snacking on tiny delicious morsels while you enjoy a variety of local wines.

Paintballing

The ultimate way to de-stress after a tricky few months of wedding planning, the bride will definitely appreciate spending a couple of hours paintballing in the wilderness.

Sangria bike tour

You've probably heard of a beer bike, but how about a sangria bike? Sip on glass after glass of Spain's most iconic alcoholic drink all while soaking up Madrid's top sights.

Graffiti workshop

Madrid's streets are famed for their colourful street art. How about creating masterpieces of your own during a group graffiti workshop?

Paella cookery class

Get your taste buds tingling with a group cookery class where an experienced Spanish chef will teach your how to whip up an authentic paella.

Watch a Real Madrid match

Gals who love watching footie should nab tickets to see Real Madrid in action at the Bernabeu Stadium. Got cash to splash? Consider VIP entry

Flamenco or salsa dancing class

Move your hips and laugh 'til you cry during a sultry salsa or flirty flamenco dance class taught by an expert.

VIP nightclub experience

Make the most of Madrid's epic nightlife with a VIP experience that'll see you skipping the line and enjoying your own reserved area in one of the city's top clubs.

Private viewing at the Prado

If the bride is a bit of an art connoisseur, she'll likely want to put Madrid's Prado Museum on the itinerary. Skip the crowds and have the entire gallery to yourself by organising a private viewing.

Vineyard visit

Spain is famed for its fruity wines. Taste some straight from its source on an incredible vineyard tour at a winery just beyond the capital.

People watch in Parque del Retiro

Enjoy a few hours outdoors at Madrid's most popular park which boasts beautiful fountains, a boating lake and the ornate Palacio del Cristal (yes, it means Crystal Palace).

Bag a bargain at the El Rastro Flea Market

If you're after souvenirs and love to haggle, Madrid's vast open-air flea market in La Latina is a must. Prefer food-related gifts? Put Mercado San Ant√≥n on your bucket lists.

Soak up some street art in La Tabacalera

Get down with the cool kids at this former tobacco factory that's been transformed into a creative mecca. View the graffiti art in the tunnels before checking out live music, dance or theatre performances.

Watch the sunset from Temple de Debod

Transferred from Egypt and rebuilt in Madrid's Cuartel de la Montana Park during the 1970s, this 2nd century BC temple is one of the most strange yet spectacular sights in the city. Its palm fringed gardens are particularly pretty at sunset.
