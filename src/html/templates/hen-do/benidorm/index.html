Whether you're party animals or simply after some sun, sea and sangria, a hen do in Benidorm could be the perfect match for a soon-to-be bride.

Once a sleepy fishing village, this seaside resort on Spain's balmy Costa Brava has earned itself a reputation for high rise hotels, cheap thrills and tacky nightlife ever since it shot to fame as a package-holiday hotspot in the 1950s. It's those factors - and, not to mention, its golden beaches and year-round warm weather - that make it such a fabulous destination for your bestie's send off. What's more, Benidorm's big British expat population really makes it feel like a (much sunnier) home away from home.

As well as dancing ‘til dawn to cheesy tunes at neon-lit nightclubs, Benidorm hen weekends can be filled with people watching along the lively promenade, Mediterranean feasts at budget-friendly tapas bars and, let’s not forget, lazy siestas in the sunshine. When it comes to those all-important hen do activities, this Spanish bolthole is unbeatable - think yacht trips, spa sessions and street parties.

The best of Benidorm

  • What's the culture like
  • Eating
  • Drinking
  • Getting around
 

Many head to Benidorm expecting a slightly balmier version of Britain. While it's true that you can get a decent cup of tea and order up a tasty full English (the ultimate hangover cure), the sunny seaside resort is also scattered with traditional tapas bars and taverns serving up regional specialties such as seafood paella.

Head to the promenade during a Benidorm hen weekend to find buzzing beach bars, karaoke venues and nightclubs that entertain the crowds into the early hours. Another popular way to let loose is at one of the many fun theme parks in the region. Feel your heart rate rise on a rollercoaster at Terra Mítica or get wet and wild at Aqualandia.

It's not all modern thrills in Benidorm, though. The historic Old Town is home to pretty cobbled streets and cute little houses, plus a picturesque harbour overlooking the charming Cala de Mal Pas. Benidorm has also been a welcoming holiday spot for the LGBT community for decades and its Old Town is where you'll find numerous fun gay bars and clubs.

 

Fish and chips? Traditional tapas? Vegan burgers? You'll be able to satisfy almost any craving in Benidorm thanks to the town's huge array of restaurants and cafes.

If you're hoping to tingle your taste buds with some Spanish fare, venture into the Old Town to sample delicious morsels like patatas bravas and succulent meatballs at Tasca y Basta. Prefer browsing multiple menus before you decide? Make a beeline for Tapas Alley (aka Calle Santa Domingo) for dozens of authentic options. You could even opt for dinner and a dance show at Benidorm Palace.

For greasy pub grub that'll remind you of home after too many sangrias, Northern Sole - also in the Old Town - serves up delicious burgers and surprisingly good fish and chips. Those more health-conscious among you might prefer the stylish blue interiors and mouth-watering vegan menu at Margem Restobar near Levante Beach.

 

As one of the most popular holiday destinations in Europe that can see upwards of 9 million tourists visiting every year, Benidorm is always packed to the rafters with keen partygoers. It's home to around 1000 different drinking venues, from traditional wine bars through to loud Irish pubs and garish karaoke spots.

For a guaranteed great night out, make your way to the so-called 'strip' along Levante Beach Boulevard for drinks and a dance at cool Pepe's. Later on, hop in a taxi to classy KU Benidorm on Av. de la Comunitat Valenciana. The huge nightclub is an offshoot of the amazing KU Ibiza and it's the best place to be after dark in Benidorm.

Benidorm's two major beaches are also dotted with fabulous venues where you can dance right on the sand, including Aloha Beach Bar that serves up tiki bar vibes with its tropical themed decor and fruity cocktails.

For something extra fancy to show your best gal just how much you love her, book a private room at Amigo's Restaurant and Roof Terrace where you can enjoy unlimited sangria and your own private (and often hunky) waiter.

 

This cheap and cheerful coastal enclave is thankfully pretty flat and spread out over only a couple of kilometres, making it totally walkable (even in heels). Bicycle hire is also popular and available at most hotels.

If the group want to head beyond Benidorm to explore stunning Spanish villages or more remote sandy coves along the Costa Brava, you'll be able to grab a great deal on car hire or a private minibus rental.

Neighbourhoods not to be missed

Levante Beach

In addition to its huge stretch of sand where you can soak up some rays (cocktail in hand, of course) or cool off with a swim in the Med, lively Levante Beach is also at the very end of Benidorm's main street of bars known locally as the 'strip'. It's definitely the place to see and be seen after dark, any night of the week.

Old Town

Benidorm isn't all tacky bars and beaches littered with half-dressed sun worshippers. The rowdy resort is also home to a picturesque Old Town that's more authentically Spanish with its white-washed houses, tapas bars and heritage sites like the Neo-Classical Church of Saint James. It's also a great area for shopping thanks to pretty lanes hiding trendy boutiques.

Poniente Beach

Benidorm's biggest beach, Playa de Poniente is over 3km long and located just west of the Old Town. Take a stroll along the rainbow-coloured, curved promenade at sunset before selecting one of its many waterfront eateries for a slap up dinner.

Hen accommodation in Benidorm

High rise hotels with purse-friendly prices are definitely up for grabs on a Benidorm hen party. Whether you're after somewhere basic, but comfortable to crash in between cocktails or you're looking to splash out on a little bit of luxury at a spa hotel, you'll find dozens of accommodation options around the town.

Hens hoping to spread out under one roof will spot several self-catering options which give you much more freedom when it comes to meals, plus amazing shared facilities like pools, gyms and games areas. You could even follow in the footsteps of the Garveys and stay at the Sol Pelícanos Ocas - the backdrop of the Solana Resort in the hilarious Benidorm TV show.
