The best of Marbella

The sundrenched Costa del Sol has long been a hotspot for a memorable European getaway - and even more so for an epic hen weekend. Marbella itself is well-known for its party culture, from the bars and clubs of the Golden Mile to party boats which take you just offshore to sip on sangria surrounded by the glistening Mediterranean Sea.

While a hen do in Marbella serves up plenty of opportunities for indulgence, the coastal resort is also a prime place for soaking up some Spanish culture. Beyond the fine sand beaches and glitzy yachts and cocktail bars of Puerto Banus, the resort is home to a handful of heritage sites. Head to the quaint Old Town to view Marbella’s 9th century castle or to enjoy some lunch at a cafe in pretty Plaza de los Naranjos (aka Orange Square) that’s famed for its ancient, fragrant orange trees.

If Marbella’s coastal position and 300+ days of annual sunshine are the biggest draws for your group, you’ll be spoilt for choice by no fewer than 23 beaches. For a lively atmosphere and a choice of thriving beach clubs where you can order up a round of cocktails (complete with mini umbrellas, of course), hit up Nikki Beach or Playa Puerto Banus. Prefer something a little more secluded? Playa de Nagueles, with its impossibly clear water, is the dream.

Then there are those all-important Marbella hen do activities. While cultural sites and lazing around on the beach will keep you occupied for a day or two, there’s also loads of water sports options, luxury spas and once-in-a-lifetime experiences on offer - think horse trekking along the dunes or tanning yourselves on the deck of a private catamaran.

 

Marbella’s status as a five-star holiday destination means there’s no shortage of incredible food to savour. Seafood is an obvious specialty, with restaurants like stylish La Sala in Puerto Banus and Restaurante Messina on the fringes of the Old Town serving up a tantalising selection of freshly caught squid, prawns and snapper.

Hens after a true taste of Andalucia should venture into the Old Town for tasty tapas at enchanting Marbella Patio right next to the castle or rich seasonal fine dining at Paco Jimenez in Orange Square.

Hen groups that want to detox after a boozy night out will love the wholesome cold pressed juices and acai bowls available at the very Instagrammable Rosas Cafe on Playa del Duque. Alternatively, head to Rachel’s Eco Love right on Playa de Nagueles for organic avocado on toast and turmeric lattes.

Finally, if the bride is impressed by culinary accolades, Marbella is home to no fewer than four Michelin star restaurants. This includes El Lago, an eatery set in the hills overlooking the resort that’s famed for its innovative tasting menus and huge selection of wine from both Spain and farther flung destinations.

 

Beach bars are big in Marbella - and you’ll find no shortage of them along the resort’s many sandy stretches. Make a beeline for the globally-loved Nikki Beach for champagne cocktails, tanning on double sized sunbeds and to attend some of the most glamourous parties on the Costa del Sol.

The Golden Mile, which stretches east from Puerto Banus to Marbella’s commercial centre, is packed with mansions, fancy resorts and many of Marbella’s super clubs. Want guaranteed entry to a high end party venue? We can organise guestlist access in advance for clubs like exclusive Pangea. Other spots in the area perfect for some late night fun include popular Linekers Bar which has VIP party packages especially for Marbella hen parties.

For a more authentically Spanish drinking experience, you’ll want to meander towards the narrow lanes of Marbella’s Old Town where you’ll find dozens of wine bars like Casa Curro Taberna (it even plays flamenco music). Gals after a well-made cocktail or G+T will then only need to walk for a few minutes towards the sea to spot the bright-red facade of cosy Barrocco Bar.

 

Marbella isn’t huge, meaning you’ll be able to explore it mostly on foot. Cycling is also popular (some hotels offer bike rentals) or you could even opt for old-school horse and carriage! For trips beyond Marbella, hire a car or get us to book a private limo or minibus depending on your hen party size.

Not keen on stumbling back to your hotel in heels after a night out? There are loads of taxi services in Marbella, however note that fare prices tend to increase after 10pm on weekdays and 9pm on weekends.

Hen activities in Marbella

Ready to party like a celeb? Or maybe the girls want a few days of R&R? Whatever the group are after, get inspired by our handful of Marbella hen do ideas below. Cocktail class

Mix up a minty mojito or stir up some sangria during a group cocktail masterclass at one of Marbella’s top bars.

Marbella VIP club night

Marbella is one of the most glam places to party in Spain and where you’ll find a whole host of cosmopolitan clubs and beachside bars. Why not dance the night away with the city’s hottest crowd by securing VIP entrance in advance?

4x4 driving tour

Test your steering skills off road with an action-packed 4x4 adventure. Split up into groups and take turns driving across rugged terrain in the foothills of the Sierra Blanca Mountains just beyond Marbella.

Water sports

Always wanted to try water skiing? Or maybe the girls fancy hopping on jet skis? Marbella’s balmy Mediterranean coastline is the perfect place to have some fun in the sun.

Beach or boat party

Get tipsy on the sand or join a boat party around the bay. Sip on ice cold beers, bubbly cava or your favourite cocktails.

Water park adventure

Get wet and wild at one of Marbella’s nearby water parks where you can whizz down steep slides, jump over waves or drift along a lazy river.

Flamenco dancing lesson

Show off your moves, learn a new skill and have an insane amount of fun during a flamenco dancing lesson.

Private yacht trip

Don’t just pose for photos next to a super yacht in Puerta Banas - hire one instead! Treat the gals like A-listers on a private catamaran trip around the Costa del Sol.

Helicopter tour

See Marbella and the stunning surrounding coastline from above when you book the hens on a fabulous helicopter tour.

Pampering spa day

Bride need some TLC before her big day? Marbella is packed to the rafters with some great spas, whether you head to one of the five-star hotels on the seafront or a day spa loved by locals.

Horse riding along the sand

Whether the group are already avid equestrians or some of you have never saddled up a horse in your life, a trek along the Spanish sand dunes is the ultimate way to end a dreamy Marbella hen weekend.

Sun yourselves on the sand

Marbella’s luxe beach clubs are legendary, however, if you’re hen do-ing on a budget, consider spreading your towels out on the sand at idyllic Playa Real de Zaragoza instead.

Explore the pretty Old Town

Despite its glitzy reputation, Marbella is actually home to a truly charming Old Town. Spend a few hours exploring (and photographing) its maze of cobbled streets and beautiful, centuries-old buildings.

Wander along the Paseo Maritimo

Enjoy fresh sea air and great sunset views as you stroll along Marbella’s paved promenade. There are also dedicated cycle lanes if you’re feeling a bit more energetic!

Visit the colourful Saturday market

Booking a hen do in Marbella over a weekend? Definitely visit Nueva Andalucia’s Saturday market located on the edge of Puerto Banus where you can pick up delicious locally grown produce - think ripe avocados, zesty oranges and juicy pomegranates.

Keys areas to check out in Marbella

Puerto Banus

Perhaps Marbella’s most luxurious neighborhood, Puerto Banus sits right on the seafront and is home to a marina filled with luxurious mega yachts. Beyond the boats, you’ll find the area is a shopaholics paradise. Head there to splurge on designer gear, to people watch or simply to soak up sensational sea views with a cocktail in hand.

Old Town

Despite its modern image, Marbella actually has a long maritime history. There’s no better place to get a taste for the resort’s centuries-old past than its charming Old Town. Picture cobbled lanes, brightly coloured period buildings and lively squares lined with friendly bars and restaurants.

Hostal Tak

Positioned along the quaint streets of Marbella’s Old Town, yet less than five minutes from the beach, Hostal Tak is a gorgeous pick for hens on a budget. Despite its name, there’s actually no shared dorms in sight. Instead, all of its double rooms have private bathrooms and feature vintage-inspired decor. Don’t miss the cool rooftop terrace that’s the perfect place for pre-girls’ night out photo ops.

The Town House

This upscale B&B sits in the very heart of the Old Town and features beautiful interiors that match the area’s rich history. Enjoy a dreamy night’s sleep in a four poster bed before tucking into a slap up breakfast in the elegant shared dining room. Most rooms additionally come with pretty, private balconies.

Hotel San Cristobal

Another Marbella Old Town gem, Hotel San Cristobal strikes the perfect balance between luxury and affordability. Split the girls up into double or twin rooms which feature white-washed walls, private bathrooms, high-speed WiFi and jaw-dropping views of either the nearby ocean or the Sierra Blanca Mountains just behind Marbella.

Barcelo Marbella

Stay only a ten minute taxi ride from the bars and clubs of Puerto Banus at this stylish hotel on the leafy outskirts of Marbella. For hens who fancy a round of golf, Barcelo Marbella is next door to the prestigious Guadalmina Golf Club. Prefer a round of drinks? Chill out poolside or on the hotel’s sunny terrace which features uninterrupted views of the green.

Hotel Don Pepe Gran Melia

If you’re looking to splurge, few hotels in Marbella are fancier than the Hotel Don Pepe. Part of the world-renowned Gran Melia group, it’s nestled right next to the ocean and within strolling distance of the Old Town. Bed down in lush king rooms and make the most of five-star facilities such as tennis courts, a spa and three outdoor swimming pools.

Boho Club 5 Stars

Prefer beautiful boutique hotels? Boho Club won’t disappoint. This five-star pick boasts magazine-worthy interiors, gorgeous garden views from every room and plenty of plush amenities. Hire bikes and cycle down to nearby Rio Verde Beach or spread out on the sunbeds around the stunning outdoor pool.

Holiday Villa

This white-washed villa in Puerto Banus is a fantastic option if you’d prefer to have your own space. Sleeping 11 hens in six bedrooms, it’s perfect for small to medium Marbella hen parties. The best part about Holiday Villa 1 has to be its outdoor space - soak up some rays by the pool in the day and chat ’til late under the stars on the spacious patio.

Holiday Villa

Perched on a hill overlooking both the ocean and Puerto Banus, Villa Sildavia is a fabulous accommodation option for groups of up to 12 hens. Sleep off your hangovers in six comfy ensuite rooms and wake up to enjoy the luxe villa’s private infinity pool, multiple terraces and gorgeous landscaped gardens.
